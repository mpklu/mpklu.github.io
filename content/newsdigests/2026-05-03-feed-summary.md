## Key Highlights
- **UiPath's CMO** argues most AI initiatives fail because pilots run isolated from business workflows — the win comes from orchestrating agents, automation, and people inside a single governed system, not deploying more tools.
- A new essay coins **"specsmaxxing"** — writing specs in YAML — as a cure for "AI psychosis" where Claude-generated code passes review but loses critical requirements when context windows reset or projects change hands.

## Analysis & Opinion
### [Exclusive: UiPath CMO Michael Atalla on AI at work](https://www.therundown.ai/p/exclusive-uipath-cmo-michael-atalla-on-ai-at-work) — Rundown
On UiPath's five-year IPO anniversary, Atalla reframes the company's pitch from task automation to orchestrating agents, automation, and humans together. His core claim: most enterprise AI fails because pilots are siloed from business goals, costs accumulate, and ROI is unmeasurable — the fix is treating agents as components of a governed workflow, with humans retaining judgment because LLMs cannot ask whether they *should* act.

### [Specsmaxxing – On overcoming AI psychosis, and why I write specs in YAML](https://acai.sh/blog/specsmaxxing) — Hacker News
The author argues we've entered a "post-slop era" of AI coding — Claude rarely produces obviously bad code anymore, but the new failure mode is context loss when sessions end or projects transfer. The proposed remedy is structured YAML specs that survive across context windows, picking up a thread mentors have pushed for decades but most developers still neglect.
