## New Products & Tools

### [Claude comes for the design stack](https://www.therundown.ai/p/claude-comes-for-the-design-stack) — Rundown
Anthropic shipped Claude Design, turning prompts, screenshots, and existing codebases into interactive prototypes and marketing assets via the Opus 4.7 vision model. During setup, Claude ingests the user's codebase and mockups to derive a brand system that auto-applies to later projects; refinement happens through chat, inline comments, direct edits, or model-generated sliders for spacing, color, and layout. Finished work hands off to Claude Code as a build-ready bundle or exports to Canva, PPTX, PDF, or standalone HTML. The timing is notable — CPO Mike Krieger resigned from Figma's board on April 14, three days before this launch — and the move continues Anthropic's consolidation of design, coding, browser automation, and office integrations into a single end-to-end product surface.

### [NVIDIA and Partners Showcase the Future of AI-Driven Manufacturing at Hannover Messe 2026](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/ai-manufacturing-hannover-messe/) — NVIDIA Blog
NVIDIA is using Hannover Messe (April 20-24) to position industrial AI as infrastructure rather than pilot-stage technology, arguing "the question is no longer whether to adopt AI, but how fast and at what scale." The centerpiece is the Industrial AI Cloud — built in Germany by Deutsche Telekom on NVIDIA hardware — pitched as a "secure, sovereign foundation" for European industrial deployment, a deliberate counter to the US-hyperscaler default. Cadence, Dassault Systèmes, Siemens, and Synopsys are integrating CUDA-X, Omniverse libraries, and Nemotron models into their EDA and CAD stacks for physics-based simulation and AI-assisted design. Read alongside the sovereignty framing: NVIDIA is making industrial AI geographically portable, which matters for customers who can't or won't route factory data through US clouds.
