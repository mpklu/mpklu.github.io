## Key Highlights
- Anti-AI activism escalates: a suspect arrested for firebombing Sam Altman's home, prompting Altman to acknowledge public anxiety about AI's societal impact
- Apple's strategic patience in the AI race may pay off as intelligence commoditizes and capable local models become viable on consumer hardware
- Kepler Communications opens the largest orbital compute cluster (40 GPUs in Earth orbit) for commercial use

## Analysis & Opinion
### [Anti-AI anger hits Sam Altman's front door](https://www.therundown.ai/p/anti-ai-anger-hits-sam-altman-front-door) — Rundown
A 20-year-old was arrested after throwing a Molotov cocktail at Sam Altman's home, believing AI would lead to human extinction. Altman responded with a personal essay acknowledging that anxiety about AI's societal impact is justified. The incident — followed by a second attack involving gunshots — highlights how anti-AI sentiment is moving from fringe concern to mainstream anger.

### [Apple's accidental moat: How the 'AI Loser' may end up winning](https://adlrocha.substack.com/p/adlrocha-how-the-ai-loser-may-end) — Hacker News
As intelligence commoditizes and the gap between frontier and open-source models collapses, Apple's restraint in the AI race becomes a strategic advantage. While competitors burned billions on infrastructure — OpenAI's Sora consumed $15M/day against $2.1M in revenue before shutdown — Apple avoided infrastructure traps, preserving optionality for a future where capable models run locally on consumer devices.

## New Products & Tools
### [The largest orbital compute cluster is open for business](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/13/the-largest-orbital-compute-cluster-is-open-for-business/) — TechCrunch
Kepler Communications is operating 40 GPUs in Earth orbit, the largest orbital compute cluster currently active. Sophia Space has signed on as a customer, demonstrating growing demand for space-based computational resources.
