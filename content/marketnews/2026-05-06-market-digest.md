## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [White House Closing on U.S.–Iran Framework, Crude Plunges](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-6-2026-updates) — TheStreet
The White House said it is "close" to a one-page memorandum of understanding with Iran to end the two-month conflict and set the framework for nuclear talks. Tehran said it is reviewing the proposal via Pakistani mediators. Equity indices ripped to new highs while Brent and WTI both plunged ~5%, unwinding the geopolitical risk premium.

### [AMD and Super Micro Earnings Reignite the AI Trade](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-tuesday-may-6-fresh-records-231128701.html) — Yahoo Finance
Advanced Micro Devices reported $10.3B in Q1 revenue (+38% YoY) with non-GAAP EPS of $1.37, sending shares up 17.77%. Super Micro (SMCI) followed with a Q3 beat on EPS and a strong Q4 outlook, surging ~20% after-hours and 5.5% in regular trading. The prints lifted NVDA (+5.27%), TSM (+5.21%), INTC, and the broader semi complex.

### [Oklo Aurora Wins NRC Principal Design Criteria Approval](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/06/oklo-advances-12-on-nrc-aurora-approval-is-this-the-ai-infrastructure-nuclear-play/) — 24/7 Wall St.
Oklo's Aurora powerhouse received NRC approval of its Principal Design Criteria, materially de-risking the regulatory path to commercialization. Combined with Oklo's 14 GW pipeline (Switch 12 GW + Equinix 500 MW), the news fueled a sector-wide nuclear melt-up: NNE +28.2%, OKLO +17.2%, SMR +14.6%.

### [Strategy Signals Tactical Bitcoin Sales](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/05/michael-saylor-s-strategy-signals-potential-bitcoin-sale-to-fund-dividends-obligations) — CoinDesk
Michael Saylor's Strategy (MSTR) booked a $12.54B Q1 net loss as BTC fell from ~$87K to ~$68K, and signaled it may now sell portions of its 818,334-BTC stack to cover $1.5B in annual dividend obligations — a marked departure from the "never sell" doctrine. Selling higher-cost-basis lots could unlock ~$2.2B in tax assets. Shares climbed 4.45%.

## Market Outlook

A new high tape underwritten by two genuine catalysts — diplomatic de-escalation with Iran and a clean AI-server earnings sweep — is a reason to respect, not fade, this rally. The internals back it: small caps participated, the VIX held a 17-handle, and the 10-year fell six basis points without breaking the equity bid. That said, the next ten sessions are dense with risk: Rocket Lab and NuScale tomorrow night, Circle and Hims on the 11th, Oklo on the 12th, and the marquee NVDA print on May 20 — any one of which could rerate sentiment around AI-infrastructure and nuclear-AI plays. With crude unwinding the war premium and the next FOMC still six weeks out, the burden of proof for the next leg sits squarely with earnings rather than with macro.
