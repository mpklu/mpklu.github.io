## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady, Signals Potential Hike Later in 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html) — CNBC
The FOMC held the funds rate at 3.50–3.75% at its June 17 meeting, but new Chair Kevin Warsh removed the easing bias from the statement and the Fed's PCE inflation projection was revised sharply higher to 3.6% from 2.7%. Median projections now point to a 3.8% funds rate by year-end — implying the next move could be a hike, not a cut. Treasury yields firmed in response, with the 10-year back near 4.51%.

### [Big Tech Hammered on AI Talent and Capex Concerns](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/21/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) — CNBC
Alphabet fell ~4.5% amid reports of senior AI talent departing for OpenAI and Anthropic, while Amazon dropped over 4% as investors questioned the return on escalating AI capital spending. The damage was concentrated in megacap platforms; semiconductors diverged sharply higher on supply-chain realignment.

### [US-Iran Peace Talks Show Major Progress](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-21/us-futures-slip-oil-climbs-on-renewed-iran-threat-markets-wrap) — Bloomberg
Both sides reported significant progress toward a peace framework targeted within two months, easing structural energy-supply risk tied to the Strait of Hormuz. Crude prices softened, with WTI down 2.1% to $74.08.

### [US-China Tariff Negotiations Center on Section 301 Rates](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation / Bloomberg
Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent indicated China would accept restoration of prior tariff rates via Section 301, while the USTR proposed a new 12.5% tariff tied to forced-labor violations. The tariff regime remains the largest US tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993.

## Market Outlook

Markets fractured along a clear fault line today: megacap tech took the brunt of the selling — Alphabet and Amazon each off more than 4% on AI talent and capex worries — dragging the Nasdaq down 1.3%, while the Dow, small caps, and a roaring semiconductor complex (SMCI +15%, INTC +5%, TSM to a new high) held the broad tape together. The divergence reflects a market re-pricing *who wins* in AI infrastructure as supply chains realign around the Apple-Intel domestic-chip partnership, even as a more hawkish Fed under new Chair Warsh — with PCE revised up to 3.6% and a potential 2026 hike now on the table — pushes the 10-year yield back toward 4.5% and pressures the highest-multiple names. The split between bulls calling for AI monetization (Ives, Lee) and skeptics warning of equity oversupply and stretched valuations (Cramer, Dalio, Buffett) captures the current standoff. With this week's PCE deflator and GDP revision landing against that hawkish backdrop, inflation data warrants close attention as the most important near-term catalyst into the July 28 FOMC.
