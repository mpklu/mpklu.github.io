## Market Outlook

Today's tape was a rotation story masquerading as a selloff: indices gave back fractions of a percent while the highest-beta corners of the watchlist — nuclear, space, and Bitcoin proxies — got hit for 5–9%, even as NVDA tagged a fresh all-time high on the Corning AI deal. With the VIX *falling* alongside equities, this looks like positioning unwind ahead of catalysts, not a regime shift. Macro is the bigger story: a 40-year-high FOMC dissent, Kashkari flagging Iran-driven uncertainty, fresh tariff fronts with Brazil and the EU, and Brent flirting with $100 are layered on top of a labor market that just printed the lowest unemployment of the year. With Friday's jobs report and Monday's CPI both within 72 hours, and NVDA earnings on May 20 anchoring a packed mid-month tape, the path of least resistance is choppy. Expect the gap between cash-flow compounders and speculative momentum to keep widening into the prints.
