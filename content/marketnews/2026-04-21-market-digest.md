### [US Launches Tariff Refund System; 330K+ Importers File Claims](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/4/20/us-launches-tariff-refund-system-as-thousands-of-importers-line-up) — Al Jazeera
The tariff refund portal went live April 20 with thousands of firms filing against up to $166B paid on 53M shipments. Global tariff remains at 10% under Section 122 until July 24, 2026, with the average effective rate at 13.7%. The refund queue offers cash-flow relief to importers but does not change the forward tariff regime that continues to pressure margins.

### [Fed Considers Rate Hikes This Year; Policymakers Shift Hawkish](https://www.usnews.com/news/business/articles/2026-04-08/more-federal-reserve-officials-see-possible-rate-hikes-this-year-minutes-show) — U.S. News & World Report
March FOMC minutes show more officials willing to consider rate *hikes* this year, up from January, with Iran-related oil pass-through to inflation as the primary concern. Consensus now points to just one cut in 2026 and another in 2027 — a materially more hawkish tilt than markets were pricing at the start of the year.

### [Iran War Threatens to Worsen Inflation via Oil and Energy Prices](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/fed-interest-rates-fomc-march) — Axios
Energy is up ~40% YTD as Middle East tensions keep a risk premium in crude. WTI jumped +4.33% today to $90.43. Roughly 68% of corporate treasurers report high geopolitical concern, reinforcing the channel from oil prices to core goods inflation that Fed officials are now openly flagging.
