## Overnight Developments

### [U.S. Strikes Iran; Tehran Declares Strait of Hormuz Closed](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2646612/amp) — Arab News / CNBC
The U.S. military launched strikes on multiple Iranian targets — reportedly firing 49 Tomahawk missiles — after President Trump vowed to respond to the downing of a U.S. Apache helicopter. In response, Iran's joint military command declared the **Strait of Hormuz closed** to oil tankers and commercial shipping, warning any vessel attempting passage would be fired on. The IEA has characterized the resulting disruption as the largest in the history of the global oil market. Brent crude is hovering near **$92/barrel**; oil pared some gains in early Thursday trade as traders weighed supply-disruption risk against headline fatigue.
Affected sectors: Energy, Defense, Airlines, Transports, Materials

### [ECB Rate Decision Due Today — 25bps Hike Near-Certain](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/stats/policy_and_exchange_rates/key_ecb_interest_rates/html/index.en.html) — European Central Bank
Markets are pricing a ~100% probability of a **25bps ECB hike to 2.25%** at today's meeting — the first hike since 2023 — driven by Middle East energy-price spikes feeding euro-area inflation. The move is fully expected, so the reaction will hinge on the accompanying guidance.
Affected sectors: Financials, Currency (EUR)
