Ives stays firmly bullish on the AI cycle, arguing the market underestimates the durability of the spend and that recent pullbacks are buying opportunities, not the start of a reversal.

## Market Outlook

Today's tape was a textbook risk rotation: the NASDAQ fell nearly 1% — led by a 3.4% drop in Apple after a underwhelming WWDC — while the DOW, small caps, and defensive UnitedHealth (a fresh 52-week high) finished higher and the VIX popped 5%. The move makes sense against a hawkish-leaning backdrop: a strong May jobs report has bond traders flirting with the idea of a rate hike, keeping the 10-year near 4.53% and pressuring long-duration growth names. With sentiment among notable voices now openly split — Tom Lee and Dan Ives still constructive, Cramer, Chamath, and Dalio flagging froth — the market lacks a clear consensus to lean on. Tomorrow's May CPI print is the catalyst that matters: a benign number would relieve the rate anxiety weighing on tech, while a hot reading warrants caution and could extend the rotation out of the highest-multiple corners of the market.
