## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [U.S.-Iran Ceasefire Triggers Broad Market Rally](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/04/08/stock-market-live-april-8-2026-sp-500-spy-soars-on-ceasefire/) — Wall Street / Bloomberg

Oil prices pulled back sharply as shipping costs via the Strait of Hormuz begin to normalize after war-risk premiums soared 1000%. Fed rate cut odds jumped from 14% to 43% on the ceasefire news.

### [Oil Prices Collapse on Geopolitical Relief](https://www.post-gazette.com/business/money/2026/04/08/stock-market-today-april-8-2026/stories/202604080042) — Pittsburgh Post-Gazette / Bloomberg

WTI crude fell sharply on the ceasefire, with energy sector stocks dropping 5-9% on the day despite ~40% YTD gains. The deflationary impulse from lower oil clashes with sticky core inflation driven by tariffs, setting up a complex backdrop for the Fed.

### [Global Tariffs Remain in Effect Until July 24, 2026](https://www.tradecomplianceresourcehub.com/2026/04/08/trump-2-0-tariff-tracker/) — Tax Foundation / Trade Compliance Hub

Trump's 10% global tariff under Section 122 remains in effect for 150 days. IEEPA tariffs were struck down by the Supreme Court on Feb 20. Tariffs estimated to increase household taxes by $600-1,500 in 2026. Trump-Xi summit scheduled for May 2026.

### [Fed Rate Cut Odds Surge to 43%](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/markets-shift-back-towards-potential-fed-rate-cut-this-year-with-iran-ceasefire-in-place.html) — CNBC

## Market Outlook

The U.S.-Iran ceasefire unleashed the strongest single-day rally in weeks, with all four major indices surging over 2.5% and the VIX collapsing 18% to 21.04 as investors rotated aggressively into risk assets. The geopolitical relief trade masks a more complex fundamental picture — oil's deflationary pullback collides with sticky tariff-driven inflation, leaving the Fed in a policy bind ahead of its April 28-29 meeting. The next two weeks are densely packed with catalysts: CPI on April 15, TSM earnings on April 16, and UNH on April 21 will test whether this rally has legs beyond the ceasefire bounce. Intel's 7.5% surge to a new 52-week high on its Fab 34 buyback signals renewed conviction in the U.S. semiconductor reshoring thesis, but the notable voices — Dimon, Buffett, Dalio, and Druckenmiller — are nearly unanimous that valuations are stretched and fragility runs deep. Watch whether the CPI print validates rate-cut pricing or snaps the narrative back to "higher for longer."
