## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [US-Iran Ceasefire Sends Oil Down ~6%](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-15/oil-holds-losses-after-iran-deal-spurs-stock-rally-markets-wrap) — Bloomberg
The US and Iran signed a memorandum of understanding for a ceasefire, with the Strait of Hormuz set to reopen Friday. The deal stripped the war-risk premium out of crude: WTI fell -5.95% to $75.89 and Brent -4.23% to $79.44, both back near pre-conflict levels. Energy and natural-gas markets sold off globally as the geopolitical bid evaporated.

### [FOMC Decision Due Tomorrow — Warsh's First Meeting](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm) — Federal Reserve
The Fed's two-day meeting (June 16–17) concludes tomorrow at 2:00 PM ET, the first chaired by Kevin Warsh. Futures price a 96–99% probability of a hold at 3.50%–3.75%, the third consecutive pause, with markets focused on the tone of guidance amid sticky inflation.

### [Trump Relaunches Tariffs via Section 301](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
After the Supreme Court blocked the emergency-powers route, the administration reopened its trade agenda using Section 301 against roughly 60 economies, including the EU. The June 11 China framework set 30% total tariffs with a 60-day pause on higher rates — the largest US tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993.

## Market Outlook

Markets split sharply today: the Dow rose 0.64% while the Nasdaq fell 1.15%, as capital rotated out of crowded AI and high-beta speculatives — nuclear names (SMR -7%, OKLO -5.5%), AI-server SMCI (-5.5% on a dilutive $7B raise), and Intel (-7% on Nvidia's PC encroachment) all sold off hard — even as oil cratered nearly 6% on the US-Iran ceasefire. The action suggests two forces converging: a geopolitical risk premium unwinding (crude back to pre-conflict levels) and a repricing of the AI-infrastructure trade for execution and dilution risk, themes echoed by cautious voices from Cramer to Dalio. With the VIX still subdued near 16, the selloff reads more like rotation than panic. All eyes now turn to tomorrow's FOMC decision under new Chair Kevin Warsh — a near-certain hold — and Thursday's CPI print, which together will determine whether this rotation hardens into something broader or proves a one-day reshuffle.
