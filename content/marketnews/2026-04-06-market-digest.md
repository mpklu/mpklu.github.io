## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Iran Ceasefire Talks: 45-Day Truce Deadline Tuesday](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-apr-6-2026-updates) — The Street
Markets rallied on reports of a 45-day truce in the Iran conflict, though Iran rejected U.S. talks after the South Pars Gas Field strike and the killing of a Revolutionary Guard intelligence chief. Trump extended the deadline to Tuesday 8pm — failure to reach a deal could reignite oil volatility and risk-off positioning.

### [Supreme Court Tariff Ruling & Trump's 10% Global Tariff](https://foreignpolicy.com/2026/04/06/trump-china-washington-trade/) — Foreign Policy / Tax Foundation
The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 that IEEPA does not authorize tariffs. Trump responded with a global 10% tariff under Section 122, effective through July 24, 2026 — the largest U.S. tax increase as a percentage of GDP since 1993. CEOs are treating tariffs as a permanent structural shift, with average household impact estimated at $1,500/year.

### [Trump-Xi Summit Set for May: Trade Negotiations](http://www.econotimes.com/Trump-Xi-Summit-2026-US-China-Trade-War-Tensions-and-Tariff-Talks-1738179) — EconoTimes
Trump is set to meet Xi Jinping in May in his first trip to China in 8 years. A prior Busan meeting produced a trade truce: U.S. scaled back tariffs, China resumed soybean purchases and paused rare earth export curbs. Markets watching for further de-escalation signals.

### [JPMorgan CEO Jamie Dimon Annual Letter Warns of Geopolitical Risks](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/06/jpmorgan-ceo-jamie-dimon-annual-letter-risks.html) — CNBC
Dimon warns the escalating Iran conflict creates "stickier" inflation and unexpectedly high interest rates, with geopolitical outcomes potentially defining the global economic order. He cautions markets are mispricing conflict risks while AI investment is structural but winners remain uncertain.

## Market Outlook

Markets edged higher Monday with the S&P 500 gaining 0.44% as investors balanced Iran ceasefire optimism against a VIX still elevated at 24.17, reflecting persistent geopolitical uncertainty. The Tuesday 8pm deadline on Iran negotiations is the week's most consequential near-term catalyst — a breakdown could send oil (WTI already at $112) sharply higher and reignite the inflation-rate narrative that Dimon and Dalio are warning about. Earnings season kicks into gear with TSM reporting April 16 and a wave of mega-caps (GOOG, INTC, GE, UNH) following the week after, offering the first real test of whether AI-driven revenue acceleration can sustain current multiples. The tariff overhang — now structural at 10% globally through July — continues to weigh on consumer-facing names like Nike and UPS, while semiconductor and AI infrastructure plays (AMD, NVDA, TSM) maintain positive momentum on institutional conviction. Watch the Iran deadline Tuesday and TSM earnings April 16 for the next directional signal.
