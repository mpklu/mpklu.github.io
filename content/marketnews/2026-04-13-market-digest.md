## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Trump Threatens 50% China Tariffs Over Iran Arms Shipment](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/13/trump-threatens-50percent-tariffs-on-china-as-report-suggests-plans-for-arms-shipment-to-iran.html) — CNBC
President Trump threatened to impose 50% tariffs on China after reports surfaced of Beijing preparing weapons shipments to Iran. The escalation comes ahead of a scheduled May 14-15 summit with Xi Jinping in Beijing. Markets shrugged off the headline, closing broadly higher, but the threat adds another layer of uncertainty to an already fraught trade relationship.

### [Fed Minutes Reveal Willingness to Consider Rate Hikes in 2026](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/fed-interest-rates-fomc-march) — Axios
March FOMC minutes show a hawkish pivot — more officials are now open to rate hikes this year, abandoning prior rate-cut expectations. The committee voted 11-1 to hold rates at 3.5%-3.75%, while raising PCE inflation projections to 2.7% (from 2.4% in December) and Core PCE to 2.7% (from 2.5%), citing energy-related supply shocks.

### [US Tariff Rate Reaches Highest Level Since 1947](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
The average effective US tariff rate has reached 10.3% in early 2026 — the highest since 1947, up from 2.4% in 2024. The Tax Foundation estimates Trump-era tariffs impose an average tax increase of $1,500 per US household.

## Market Outlook

Markets rallied broadly on Monday with the Russell 2000 leading at +1.52%, suggesting risk appetite remains intact despite mounting tariff rhetoric and a hawkish Fed pivot. WTI crude tumbled 6.4% — the sharpest single-day decline in months — easing near-term inflation pressure even as the Fed's upwardly revised PCE projections (2.7%) loom over the rate outlook. Intel's parabolic run to a new 52-week high (+4.5% today, +53% over 9 sessions) and Circle's 12% surge underscore how quickly momentum can reshape individual names. With PPI data due tomorrow, TSM earnings on Thursday, and a dense reporting slate kicking off next week — INTC (Apr 23), UNH (Apr 21), then GOOG/META/AAPL/AMZN the week after — the next two weeks will test whether this rally has fundamental legs or is simply a positioning reflex ahead of the April 28-29 FOMC meeting.
