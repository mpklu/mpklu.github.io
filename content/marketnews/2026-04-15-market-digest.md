## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [S&P 500 Breaks Above 7,000 to New All-Time High](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-apr-15-2026-futures-flat-sp-500-close-to-all-time-high) — TheStreet
The S&P 500 surpassed its January record of 7,002.28 and closed at 7,022.95, a new all-time high. Tech and financials led the charge, with NASDAQ surging 1.59%. The milestone reflects renewed risk appetite as geopolitical tensions show signs of easing.

### [Trump Threatens 50% Tariffs on China Over Iran Arms Report](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/13/trump-threatens-50percent-tariffs-on-china-as-report-suggests-plans-for-arms-shipment-to-iran.html) — CNBC
President Trump threatened 50% tariffs on China in response to Beijing's alleged plans to ship weapons to Iran. A Xi-Trump summit is scheduled for May 14-15 in Beijing, which markets are watching as a potential de-escalation catalyst.

### [Iran Conflict De-escalation Signals Boost Markets](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/13/business/strait-of-hormuz-blockade-trump-iran-tariffs) — CNN
Trump indicated a possible wind-down of the Iran conflict, supporting the equity rally. WTI crude has fallen sharply from recent highs on de-escalation expectations, easing one of the market's biggest risk factors.

### [Inflation Hits 3.3% — Gasoline Surges 21.2%](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/03/trump-tariffs-trade-war-impact.html) — CNBC
The latest CPI reading shows inflation at 3.3%, the fastest pace in nearly two years, driven by a 21.2% surge in gasoline prices. Tariff-driven cost increases have averaged $1,500 per U.S. household in 2026. Canada has paused tariffs on U.S. imports for manufacturing and food processing for six months.

## Market Outlook

The S&P 500 punched through 7,000 to a new all-time high, led by a broad-based rally spanning AI healthcare (TEM +13.8%, HIMS +13.3%), nuclear energy (SMR +12.1%), and fintech (SOFI +3.5%) — though the Dow's 0.15% dip signals this isn't an indiscriminate bid. Iran de-escalation hopes and strong bank earnings provided the fuel, but 3.3% inflation and looming 50% China tariff threats create a tense backdrop that warrants caution at record levels. TSMC's earnings tomorrow morning will be the first major test of AI capex durability this season, with Alphabet, Meta, and Intel all on deck within two weeks — any disappointment on AI spending commitments could quickly test the market's conviction above 7,000.
