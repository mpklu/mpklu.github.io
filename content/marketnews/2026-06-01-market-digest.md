## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Nvidia's New PC Chip Drives Tech Rally; S&P 500 Hits All-Time High](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-01-2026) — TheStreet
NVIDIA unveiled its Arm-based RTX Spark superchip for PCs at COMPUTEX, sending its shares up ~5.6% and lifting PC hardware names (Dell +10%, HP +8%). The flip side: Intel fell ~5% as the launch directly threatens its CPU franchise. The day's record closes were powered almost entirely by this single catalyst.

### [Fed Holds Steady; Rising PCE Inflation Curtails Rate-Cut Outlook](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/fed-reserve-rate-hikes) — CNBC
With headline PCE running near 3.5% year-over-year, the Fed is maintaining a high bar for cuts. BofA now expects rates to stay on hold through 2026, with easing potentially deferred to 2027. This keeps the upcoming jobs data and the June FOMC firmly in focus.

### [U.S.–Iran Ceasefire Remains Fragile; Oil Stays Elevated](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-31/oil-climbs-as-us-iran-ceasefire-remains-elusive-markets-wrap) — Bloomberg
More than five weeks after the announced ceasefire, shipping through the Strait of Hormuz remains at ~5% of pre-conflict levels. WTI jumped +2.7% to ~$92 and Brent +2.0% to ~$95 as supply concerns persist despite prices holding below $100.

## Market Outlook

Major indices closed at fresh records — the S&P 500 at 7,600 and the NASDAQ above 27,000 — but the move was carried almost entirely by NVIDIA's +5.6% surge on its COMPUTEX RTX Spark reveal, while the Russell 2000 fell and the VIX rose +4.6%. That divergence matters: the same chip launch that lifted NVIDIA and supplier TSMC (+3.9% to a new 52-week high) hammered Intel (-5.2%), and speculative high-flyers from Rocket Lab (-14%) to Strategy (-5.8%) and Circle (-6.9%) sold off hard, suggesting investors are rotating within the AI trade rather than buying the market broadly. With PCE inflation near 3.5% and the Fed signaling a high bar for cuts, breadth this thin warrants caution rather than chase. The most important catalyst now is Friday's May payrolls report (forecast 102K, unemployment expected to tick up to 4.4%), which — ahead of the June 16-17 FOMC — will shape whether the rate-cut timeline shifts at all.
