## Overnight Developments

### [Global Semiconductor Selloff Accelerates; Nikkei Falls 4%](https://invezz.com/en-ae/news/2026/07/20/nikkei-225-slides-as-softbank-tokyo-electron-and-kioxia-stocks-sink/) — Invezz
The Nikkei 225 plunged **-4.14%** to 64,141 in a chip-led rout, now down roughly 11% from its June peak. Kioxia (-16%), SoftBank (-9%), Tokyo Electron (-8%) and Advantest (-7.2%) led losses as AI valuation concerns deepened globally. South Korea's Kospi fell a similar -4.9% (Samsung -4.4%, SK Hynix -3.3%). Watch NVDA, SMCI, INTC, TSM and the broader chip complex at open.
Affected sectors: Semiconductors, Technology, AI-related

### [US-Iran Military Escalation Intensifies](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/20/middleeast/iran-us-escalation-conflict-intl) — CNN
The US-Iran conflict entered a critical phase with a ninth night of strikes and expanded Iranian missile attacks across multiple countries. Oil spiked ~2%, with Brent trading near $90 on fears of Strait of Hormuz tanker disruptions. Energy and defense names could catch a bid; airlines and shipping are exposed to fuel-cost and route risk.
Affected sectors: Energy, Defense, Shipping, Aviation

### [USTR Section 301 Tariff Decision Due Today](https://www.tariffstool.com/guides/section-122-expires-july-24-2026-importer-playbook) — Tariff Tool
The USTR faces a completion deadline for Section 301 investigations proposing 12.5% duties on 46 countries, including China, Vietnam, India, Japan and South Korea. The decision sets tariff rates ahead of the July 24 Section 122 expiration; a delay would trigger a temporary MFN reversion with cascading supply-chain effects.
Affected sectors: Consumer Goods, Semiconductors, Manufacturing, Industrials
