### [Trump Extends US-Iran Ceasefire Indefinitely](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-april-22-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-trump-us-iran-ceasefire-230429476.html) — Yahoo Finance

### [US Tariff Refund System Goes Live](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/4/20/us-launches-tariff-refund-system-as-thousands-of-importers-line-up) — Al Jazeera
Over 56,000 importers have filed for electronic refunds totaling roughly $127B (76% of eligible amount) following the Supreme Court ruling on illegally collected tariffs. The universal 10% tariff remains in effect until July 24, 2026, but the refund flow is a meaningful working-capital tailwind for retailers and industrials with large import exposure.

### [Dimon Warns Iran War Risks Sticky Inflation in 2026 Letter](https://www.financialcontent.com/article/marketminute-2026-4-6-dimons-2026-shareholder-letter-war-inflation-and-the-skunk-at-the-party) — FinancialContent

He called high asset prices the "skunk at the party" — a caution worth registering as indices push all-time highs.

## Market Outlook

Today's move was not just a melt-up — it was an Iran-ceasefire relief rally layered on top of genuine earnings follow-through, with UNH's beat, TSMC's raised AI guide, and a White-House-driven nuclear bid (NNE, SMR, OKLO all +12%+) doing the heavy lifting beneath a 1.6% NASDAQ gain. Underneath the surface, though, oil's 3.6% rise in WTI and Brent's 9% spike suggests the market is pricing in *both* risk-off relief *and* a post-ceasefire supply-chain reset, a tension worth watching. Marks, Buffett, and Dimon all flagged valuation concern this week even as Lee and Ives called for more upside — a healthy split that typically marks tactically-bullish-but-structurally-cautious regimes. The next eight trading days are the real test: INTC prints tomorrow, UPS on the 28th, and AMZN/GOOG/META/SOFI all report Apr 29 — enough megacap breadth to either validate the AI-capex narrative or crack it. With breakeven inflation ticking back to 2.38% and the 10Y stuck at 4.30%, watch the PCE deflator on Apr 30 as the most important single data print of the coming week.
