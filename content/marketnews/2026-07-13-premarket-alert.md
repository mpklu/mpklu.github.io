## Overnight Developments

### [US and Iran Trade Attacks Over the Strait of Hormuz](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/13/oil-prices-jump-as-us-and-iran-trade-attacks-over-strait-of-hormuz) — Al Jazeera / BNN Bloomberg

The US launched several waves of strikes on Iran into Monday morning after an Iranian attack on a container ship in the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend left the vessel ablaze and a crew member missing. Iran retaliated with missiles and drones targeting countries across the Gulf and declared the Strait closed "until further notice" — a claim US Central Command has rejected. Brent crude jumped roughly **4% to ~$78.85/barrel**, its highest since June 22, and maritime traffic through the world's most important oil chokepoint has fallen sharply.

Affected sectors: Energy (bullish), Shipping/Logistics, Defense, broad risk-off.

### International Markets
Asia sold off on the escalation — Nikkei 225 **-1.79%**, Shanghai Composite **-2.09%** — while Hang Seng held slightly positive (+0.16%). Europe is roughly flat: FTSE 100 -0.13%, DAX +0.06%.

## What to Watch at Open

Watch crude oil and any headlines confirming or denying an actual Strait of Hormuz closure — that single variable drives the risk tone; a genuine shutdown would send oil sharply higher and pressure equities, while continued open transit keeps the initial equity dip contained.
