### [Steel, Aluminum, Copper Tariff Adjustments Take Effect April 6](https://kpmg.com/us/en/taxnewsflash/news/2026/04/united-states-new-rules-calculating-section-232-tariffs-steel-aluminum-copper.html) — KPMG

One year after "Liberation Day," tariffs represent the largest US tax increase as a percentage of GDP since 1993, averaging $1,500 per household in 2026.

## Market Outlook

Markets traded in a tight range as investors digested a blockbuster March jobs report (178K vs. 65K expected) alongside a 6.5% surge in WTI crude to $111.54 on Middle East escalation — a textbook stagflationary cocktail. The strong labor data effectively takes a near-term Fed cut off the table, with the next FOMC meeting on April 28 likely a hold at 3.50–3.75%, while oil-driven inflation and new Section 232 tariffs taking effect Sunday add to the cost pressure. Small caps outperformed (+0.70%) as Russell 2000 benefited from the domestic hiring strength, while the Dow lagged on GE Aerospace's 4% drop. With earnings season kicking off in two weeks — TSM on April 16, followed by a wave of mega-cap tech reports — the market's ability to sustain current levels depends on whether corporate profits can absorb the dual headwinds of $110+ oil and escalating trade costs.
