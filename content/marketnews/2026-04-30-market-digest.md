## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed holds rates at 3.5–3.75% in Powell's final meeting as chair](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/fed-interest-rate-decision-april-2026.html) — CNBC
The FOMC kept the target range unchanged on April 29 but the meeting was unusually contentious, with multiple dissents tied to elevated inflation and Middle East energy uncertainty. Powell confirmed he will step aside as chair effective May 15 while remaining on the Board, setting up a leadership transition just as policymakers debate whether persistent oil-driven inflation forces a hawkish turn.

### [Brent crude pushes past $111 as US–Iran tensions escalate](https://sundayguardianlive.com/business/us-stock-market-today-30-april-2026-dow-falls-nasdaq-sp-500-edge-higher-as-oil-surges-above-114-on-us-iran-tensions-gold-silver-climb-bitcoin-retreats-76k-what-investors-should-watch-187643) — Sunday Guardian
Reports that the Trump administration is reviewing military options around the Strait of Hormuz pushed energy markets sharply higher this week before today's reversal, with WTI still elevated near $105 and Brent above $111. Energy volatility — not earnings — remains the dominant macro variable, and gold's break above $4,600 underscores how much hedging demand the geopolitical premium is generating.

### [Trump escalates tariff threats: 50% on metals, UK digital services](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
The administration's effective tariff rate now sits at 11.8% after recent escalations, with 50% flat-rate threats on aluminum, steel, and copper articles, plus retaliatory rhetoric over the UK's digital services tax. Several earlier tariffs have been invalidated by the Supreme Court, but the trajectory still tilts toward higher input costs for US manufacturers heading into Q2.

## Market Outlook

A breakout close above S&P 7,200 with the VIX collapsing 10% looks unambiguously bullish on the surface, but the day's biggest tape stories — Meta's CapEx-driven 9% slide earlier in the week, NVIDIA's 5.5% drop on Google opening direct TPU sales, and a rotation into nuclear (OKLO, SMR) and defense-adjacent names — suggest leadership is rotating, not collapsing. The Fed's contentious hold under Powell's final meeting, paired with Brent still above $111 and Dimon's bond-crisis warning, means the macro backdrop is far less benign than the index print implies. Watch the May 1–3 jobs/ISM cluster and a heavy watchlist earnings week (PFE, MSTR, SMCI, TEM all on May 5) — those prints will determine whether the breakout has staying power or whether the divergence between index strength and AI-capex fatigue forces a reset. With 12 of 23 watchlist names reporting inside two weeks, single-stock dispersion warrants more attention than directional macro positioning right now.
