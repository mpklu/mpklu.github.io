Stocks sold off across the board on Fed day. Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting delivered a hold but a hawkish-leaning dot plot, and risk came off fast: every major index finished red, the VIX jumped more than 12% back above 18, and crowded tech names led the slide. This wasn't a rotation like yesterday — it was broad de-risking ahead of the policy statement.

## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed Holds at 3.50%–3.75%, Dot Plot Turns Hawkish](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-meeting-today-live-updates.html) — CNBC
Kevin Warsh chaired his first FOMC meeting, leaving the target range unchanged at 3.50%–3.75% in a unanimous vote. The surprise was the projections: with PCE inflation expectations revised up to roughly 3.6% (from 2.7% in March) on Iran-conflict energy prices, several members now see policy staying tighter for longer rather than easing into year-end. The statement was notably shorter and dispensed with explicit forward guidance — a stylistic break that left markets to interpret intent, and they read it cautiously.

### [Trump Relaunches Tariff War via Section 301 on 80+ Countries](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/15/how-trump-is-relaunching-a-tariff-war-citing-forced-labour-concerns) — Al Jazeera
The USTR determined that 60+ economies (including the EU, 80+ countries in total) failed to enforce restrictions on forced-labor goods, opening Section 301 actions. A new 25% tariff was proposed on Brazilian footwear, apparel, and travel products. The China framework holds at 30% total tariffs (20% "fentanyl" + 10% "reciprocal") through a 60-day pause. The escalation adds to the inflation-expectations problem the Fed just flagged.

### [Iran Conflict Keeps Geopolitical Risk Premium Elevated](https://www.weforum.org/publications/global-risks-report-2026/digest/) — World Economic Forum
The Iran conflict remains a live energy-security risk through the Strait of Hormuz, feeding directly into the Fed's upgraded inflation path. Crude held firm (WTI +0.61% to $75.26, Brent +0.50% to $78.83) as the war premium lingers, and the situation is increasingly drawing 1970s-style energy-shock comparisons.

## Market Outlook

Markets de-risked sharply on Fed day: every major index closed lower and the VIX spiked 12% above 18 after Kevin Warsh's first FOMC held rates steady but lifted its inflation projections to ~3.6%, signaling tighter-for-longer policy as Iran-driven energy prices and a fresh round of Section 301 tariffs feed the inflation outlook. The damage was broadest in crowded tech and high-beta speculatives — META -5%, SMCI -5.7%, MSTR -5.2% — while nuclear names, Intel, and defensive healthcare held up, a sign that leadership is narrowing rather than the whole tape rolling over. With no FOMC meeting until July 28, the near-term tone now hinges on incoming data and whether the inflation-expectations spike proves sticky. Thursday's jobless claims and the durability of crude's geopolitical premium warrant attention as the market recalibrates to a Fed that just told it not to count on cuts.
