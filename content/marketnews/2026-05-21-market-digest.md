## Market Outlook

A subtly broad day under the surface — the Dow's record close, a near-1% Russell move, and a 4% VIX drop on U.S.–Iran de-escalation outweighed the small headline gain in the S&P. Capital is rotating: small-caps and nuclear/quantum thematics led (SMR +9.8%, OKLO +5.6%, HIMS +5.2%, CRCL +3.8%), while NVDA was flat-to-down the day after a blowout, consistent with Druckenmiller's documented trim of mega-cap AI exposure. With the next FOMC still 26 days out and BoA pushing cuts into H2 2027, the near-term tape is being shaped by tariff timing (Section 122 expires July 24 absent congressional action) and AI infrastructure capex more than by monetary policy. Next week the focus shifts to Costco earnings on May 28 — a useful read on the consumer with the stock trading within 5% of its 52-week high — and any movement on the U.S.–Iran framework that drove today's risk-on tone.
