## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [US imposes 25% tariffs on Brazil, effective July 22](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/16/business/us-brazil-tariffs-trade-intl-hnk) — CNN Business
Washington announced 25% tariffs on Brazilian imports citing unfair trade practices, taking effect July 22. Coffee, beef, oranges, oil/gas, and aerospace parts are exempted. Brazil's Lula pledged countermeasures and WTO action — a fresh trade-war flashpoint that added to the day's risk-off tone.

### [July rate-hike odds climbing toward ~46%](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/-a-july-rate-hike-from-the-fed-the-odds-are-rising.html) — CNBC
CME FedWatch pricing now puts a ~46.5% probability on a 25bp hike at the July 28–29 FOMC. The Fed held at 3.50%–3.75% in June and signaled a possible hike later in 2026; firmer oil on Iran tensions has nudged the odds higher. A live meeting so close to earnings season warrants attention.

### [Chip stocks sell off as TSMC lifts capex](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-thursday-july-16-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-103116735.html) — Yahoo Finance
Semiconductors led the decline after Taiwan Semiconductor raised its 2026 capex guide to $60–64B (from $52–56B) despite a blowout quarter. AI-infrastructure jitters spread across the complex, offset partly by UnitedHealth's earnings beat.

Cramer sees an AI dot-com-style crash as unlikely absent major rate hikes, noting the S&P's ~20x forward multiple sits well below 2000's 25x+. He cautioned that midweek's tech rally may have run ahead of fundamentals and worries a flood of new equity and bond issuance could pressure balance.

### [Nasdaq 30,000 within 6–9 months](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2026/07/01/watch-cnbcs-full-interview-with-tech-strategist-dan-ives.html) — Dan Ives, Wedbush (2026-07-01)
Ives frames the recent ~$3T tech selloff as a buying opportunity, naming Microsoft, Oracle, and Alphabet as oversold. He expects AI monetization to become visible in Q2 earnings and sees ~15% more upside for tech into year-end.

## Market Outlook

Equities sold off broadly as Taiwan Semiconductor's record quarter came paired with a sharply higher capex guide ($60–64B), spooking margin-sensitive investors and dragging the Nasdaq down 1.47% while the VIX jumped nearly 7%. The pain concentrated in high-beta AI, nuclear, and space names — RKLB (-10.7%), NNE (-9.4%), HIMS (-9.1%), and OKLO (-8.4%) all cratered, with several nuclear plays printing fresh 52-week lows — even as staples and healthcare (COST +3.1%, UNH +1.9% on a 31% earnings beat) and a new-high AAPL cushioned the tape. The backdrop turned less friendly on two fronts: fresh 25% tariffs on Brazil and rising odds (~46%) of a July 28–29 rate hike, which lifted the 10-year to 4.57% and suggests caution toward the most rate-sensitive growth. Momentum near-term favors quality and cash flow over speculative story stocks. The most important catalyst ahead is the FOMC decision in 12 days, bracketed by a dense earnings slate that kicks off with Alphabet on July 22 and Intel on July 23.
