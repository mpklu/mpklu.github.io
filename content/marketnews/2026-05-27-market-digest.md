## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [EU Approves US Trade Deal Implementation Amid Tariff Threats](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/05/20/eu-approves-trade-deal-with-the-us-despite-uncertainty-in-transatlantic-relations) — Euronews
The EU reached agreement to implement the US trade deal eliminating duties on most US industrial goods, concluding negotiations after Trump threatened 25% tariffs on EU cars by July 4. The Commission retains authority to suspend the deal if the US fails to lift tariffs on European steel and aluminum by year-end 2026.

### [US-China Tariff Deal Maintains 30% Combined Tariffs](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/u-s-and-china-seek-to-repair-damage-from-tariff-war-that-sent-trade-into-a-freefall) — PBS News
The Trump-China deal maintains current 20% fentanyl and 10% reciprocal tariffs while pausing a planned increase to 125% for another 90 days. Aggregate Trump tariffs now represent the largest US tax increase as a percent of GDP since 1993, averaging roughly $1,500 per household in 2026.

### [Kevin Warsh Sworn In as Fed Chairman Amid Rate Cut Debate](https://tradingeconomic.com/united-states/interest-rate) — Federal Reserve
Kevin Warsh took the oath of office as Fed chairman and FOMC chair. The Fed kept the policy rate at 3.50%–3.75% with a notable 8-4 dissent — the deepest split since 1992 — and markets now price lower odds of further cuts in 2026 as inflation remains sticky.

### [Oil Retreats Below $90 on Iran Deal Prospects](https://www.cnbc.com/) — CNBC
US oil fell below $90/barrel on reports that a possible Iran agreement could restore Hormuz traffic. The Dow jumped nearly 300 points intraday to record territory on declining energy prices and improved sentiment.

## Market Outlook

Major indices finished essentially flat with the Dow's +0.36% gain again leading, as a ~3% slide in oil on Iran-deal optimism boosted broader sentiment but failed to translate into breadth — small caps drifted lower and the S&P closed up just one point. Underneath the surface, leadership is narrowing back into the megacap AI complex: META +4%, AMZN near 52-week highs, and TSM and RKLB tagging new highs while VIX dropped to 16.29, a combination that warrants attention given Cramer and Dimon's separate warnings about concentrated risk and stretched valuations. The Fed's recent 8–4 dissent under new Chair Warsh signals a more contested policy path even as the funds rate sits at 3.64% with breakevens steady at 2.4%, leaving real yields — not headline policy — as the swing factor for equity multiples. Costco reports tomorrow with a high bar already priced in (P/E 52), and the same morning brings the Q1 GDP second estimate (consensus 0.5% vs. prior 2.0%) plus Personal Spending — a sharp downward revision would test the soft-landing narrative just as Tom Lee's summer-correction scenario starts to look less academic. Momentum favors the AI complex into month-end, but the calendar suggests the next 48 hours could redefine the tone.
