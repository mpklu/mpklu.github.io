## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [NVIDIA Beats Earnings, Raises Guidance; S&P 500 Climbs 0.32%](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-nasdaq-dow-jones-sp-500-updates-may-20-2026) — TheStreet
NVIDIA reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of **$81.62B (+85% YoY)** with **$91B Q2 guidance** and authorized an **$80B buyback** alongside a quarterly dividend bump to 25 cents. Despite the headline beat, shares dipped in after-hours trading, suggesting expectations had been priced in.

### [Trump-Xi Summit Signals Trade War De-escalation](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/14/trump-xi-summit-us-china-trade-taiwan-iran-nvidia.html) — CNBC
Last week's Beijing summit produced incremental progress on agricultural and industrial purchases, with Taiwan, rare earths, and Nvidia chip exports all on the table. Markets are reading the meeting as an extension of the existing trade truce — bullish for semis but conditional.

### [Iran Revolutionary Guard Threatens Regional Escalation](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-nasdaq-dow-jones-sp-500-updates-may-20-2026) — TheStreet
Iran's IRGC warned it would expand Middle East conflict "beyond the region" if U.S. and Israel resume strikes. Oil sold off **~5%** today despite the threat — markets are discounting follow-through, but the headline risk premium remains elevated.

### [FOMC Held at 3.50–3.75% With First Dissent in 34 Years](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The April 29 hold marked the third straight pause and produced an 8-4 vote — the first dissent since October 1992. BofA pushed its first-cut forecast to Q3 2027. Markets remain stuck between sticky inflation and an incoming Chair (Kevin Warsh) before the June meeting.

## Market Outlook

Today's tape was a textbook risk-on day: Dow through 50K, Russell ripping 2.6%, VIX cooling under 18, and a clean breadth profile across nuclear (OKLO +12%, NNE +8%), AI infrastructure (SMCI +9%, INTC +6%), and space defense (RKLB +6.5%). NVIDIA's $91B guide and $80B buyback validated the capex narrative — but the muted after-hours reaction tells you how much of that beat was already in the cake. Underneath it, the gold/oil split (gold +1.8%, crude -5%) is the more interesting tell: macro money is hedging debasement, not war. With FOMC minutes hitting tonight and Philly Fed plus jobless claims tomorrow, the near-term setup hinges on whether the data give a re-accelerating Fed any reason to soften the "higher-for-longer" framing — and on whether the breadth that drove today's rally can survive any whiff of a hawkish tone.
