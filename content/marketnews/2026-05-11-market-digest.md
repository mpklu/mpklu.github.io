## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Iran War Escalation Pushes Oil Prices Sharply Higher](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-may-11-oil-rise-trump-rejects-peace-225625559.html) — Yahoo Finance

Brent briefly surged ~3.5% intraday before paring; WTI closed at $98.48 (-1.46%) but Brent held near $104. The risk premium in crude is back on the board, and the geopolitical overhang now bleeds directly into the CPI print due Wednesday.

### [US Trade Court Strikes Down Trump's 10% Global Tariffs](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/05/07/tariffs-trade-court-ruling-trump/) — Washington Post
The Court of International Trade ruled 2-1 against Trump's blanket 10% tariffs imposed in February, citing missing statutory conditions. An estimated $166B in collected tariffs is eligible for refunds. The administration is exploring alternative statutes, leaving trade policy a live wire heading into earnings season.

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady at 3.50–3.75% Amid Persistent Inflation](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressrelease/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The FOMC held for a third consecutive meeting with a record four dissents. Bank of America now sees no rate cuts until H2 2027. Kevin Warsh takes the chair by mid-June, adding succession risk to a Fed already split.

### [Stock Market Reaches All-Time Highs Despite Geopolitical Concerns](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-may-11-oil-rise-trump-rejects-peace-225625559.html) — Yahoo Finance

## Market Outlook

Records on the headline indices belied a split tape today: nuclear, space, and crypto-infrastructure ripped (RKLB +10.7%, CRCL +13.6%, OKLO +7.5%, SMR +7.0%) while mega-cap tech mostly leaked (GOOG -2.3%, META -1.8%, AMZN -1.4%) and the VIX jumped nearly 7%. The bid is rotating, not retreating — money is moving down the AI value chain into power, infrastructure, and stablecoin rails, the same trade Chamath and Dan Ives describe from opposite ends. With Wednesday's CPI expected to print **3.8% vs 3.3% prior** and Nvidia's earnings landing May 20, the calm on the index level is doing real work hiding the underlying repositioning. Watch the CPI surprise direction: a hot print likely cements the BofA "no cuts until H2 2027" view that's already in the curve, while any cooler reading reopens the rate-cut narrative the dissenting FOMC hawks are working to close.
