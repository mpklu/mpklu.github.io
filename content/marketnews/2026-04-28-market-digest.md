### [OpenAI Misses Revenue Targets, Sparks Tech Selloff](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/27/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) — CNBC/WSJ

The episode reignites concerns about the durability of AI capex assumptions ahead of mega-cap prints this week.

### [Fed Holds Steady at April 29 Decision; Powell's Final Meeting](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/fed-rate-decision-april-2026-powell-final-meeting/) — Federal Reserve
The FOMC meets Wednesday with consensus expecting a third consecutive pause at 3.50%–3.75%. It is also Jerome Powell's last meeting before his term ends May 15; Kevin Warsh has been nominated as successor. The 2pm ET statement and press conference will be parsed for tone on the disinflation path and any acknowledgement of recent oil-price strength.

### [Oil Surges on Geopolitical Tensions; Gas Hits $4.18](https://angle360ng.com/dow-jones-today-live-april-28-2026/) — AAA/CME
WTI crossed $100 intraday and settled at $99.54 (+2.0%); Brent reached $103.93 (+1.2%). Retail gasoline at $4.176 is the highest since August 2022. OPEC's signal of cautious production strategy keeps the supply story tight, complicating the Fed's inflation glide path heading into Wednesday's decision.

### [Tariff Refund System Strained as $166B in IEEPA Refunds Begins](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/4/20/us-launches-tariff-refund-system-as-thousands-of-importers-line-up) — U.S. Court of International Trade
The court-ordered IEEPA refund process launched April 20 via the CAPE portal, but importers are reporting bottlenecks, slow loads, and errors. The global effective tariff rate stands at 11.8%, with the 10% Section 122 tariff in force through July 24. Costco was among the names today flagged as facing legal/political questions over how it will handle pass-through refunds.

### [Canada Elects Carney; Tariff Standoff Deepens](https://www.clickorlando.com/news/local/2026/04/28/dollars-sense-the-tariff-house-of-cards-just-collapsed-now-what/) — Election Day Coverage
Mark Carney's Liberals won a minority government on April 28; Carney pledged a tougher stance on Trump tariff demands. The cross-border friction is a medium-term headwind for industrial supply chains and adds to the macro uncertainty backdrop.

Dimon's renewed credit-cycle warning and Dalio's "bubble" framing suggest that the disconnect between record-high indices and softening AI assumptions warrants attention. With WTI back through $100 and the 10Y at 4.35%, any hawkish wobble from Wednesday's Fed statement could compound the AI pressure, while a clean mega-cap earnings beat could quickly restore the bid. The next 72 hours are the year's highest-information moment — risk control over conviction.
