## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [CPI Surges to 3.4% YoY — Headline Miss Rattles Markets](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-apr-10-2026-dow-futures-flat-on-shaky-iran-ceasefire) — TheStreet
March headline CPI came in at +0.9% MoM (vs 0.7% estimate), pushing year-over-year inflation to **3.4%** — up sharply from 2.4%. Core CPI was slightly better at +0.2% vs 0.3% consensus, but the headline miss signals persistent inflationary pressure that complicates the Fed's rate path. The Dow shed 269 points as markets repriced rate-cut expectations.

### [Fed Officials Open Door to Rate Hikes](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/top-fed-official-sees-potential-rate-hike-amid-higher-gas-prices-inflation-concerns) — PBS News
March FOMC minutes revealed officials are actively considering interest rate hikes if inflation remains persistently above the 2% target. The current fed funds rate sits at 3.50–3.75%. Despite hawkish signals, consensus still prices in one rate cut for 2026 — but today's CPI print adds weight to the hike scenario heading into the April 28–29 meeting.

### [Consumer Sentiment Hits Record Low at 52.1](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-apr-10-2026-dow-futures-flat-on-shaky-iran-ceasefire) — TheStreet
The University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment Index dropped to **52.1** (preliminary) from 54.1, reflecting deteriorating confidence amid inflation concerns, tariff uncertainty, and geopolitical tensions.

### [Court Challenge to Trump's 10% Tariffs — Oral Arguments Today](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/4/10/us-federal-court-hears-new-case-against-trump-tariffs) — Al Jazeera
A three-judge panel of the US Court of International Trade heard oral arguments on the 10% global import tax covering ~$1.2 trillion (34%) of annual imports. Democratic-led states and small businesses argue the tariffs sidestep the Supreme Court's February ruling. The [Tax Foundation](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) estimates the tariff translates to an average **$1,500 annual household tax increase**.

### [Iran Ceasefire Uncertainty Pressures Markets](https://www.post-gazette.com/business/money/2026/04/10/stock-market-today-april-10-2026/) — Pittsburgh Post-Gazette
VP JD Vance departed for Islamabad to join US-Iran negotiations scheduled for Saturday. The Trump administration is preparing military contingencies if talks fail, adding geopolitical risk premium to an already jittery market.

## Market Outlook

Markets diverged on a turbulent Friday, with the NASDAQ eking out a **+0.35%** gain on semiconductor strength (NVDA +3.1%, SMCI +8.4%, TSM +1.2%) while the Dow shed **269 points** as a hotter-than-expected CPI print — **3.4% YoY** versus 2.4% prior — forced traders to reprice the Fed's rate path. The inflation surprise, paired with consumer sentiment hitting a record low of 52.1, makes the April 28–29 FOMC meeting a potential inflection point, with officials now openly discussing rate hikes rather than cuts. Geopolitical risk remains elevated with Iran ceasefire negotiations on a knife's edge heading into Saturday, and the ongoing tariff legal battle adding fiscal uncertainty. TSMC's April 16 earnings report will be the week's first major test of whether AI-driven demand remains resilient enough to offset macro headwinds, followed by a massive earnings gauntlet from April 21–30 featuring INTC, UNH, GOOG, META, SOFI, AAPL, and AMZN.
