## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Iran Escalation Roils Markets — Oil Volatile on New Airstrikes](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-july-20-2026) — TheStreet
A fresh round of U.S. airstrikes against Iran — reportedly with an American military casualty — and a Houthi maritime embargo against Saudi Arabia sent oil whipsawing. WTI settled around $82 after touching much higher on the initial scare, with inventories reported near multi-decade lows. Energy risk premium is back in focus.

### [Tariff War Escalates — U.S.–China Trade at Decade Low](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
U.S. tariffs on China are holding near 48% after peaking in triple digits during 2025, with fresh 25% levies reinstated on EU vehicles and 50% on steel/aluminum. The Tax Foundation estimates the tariff regime is the largest U.S. tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993 — roughly $1,500 per household — and U.S. soybean exports to China fell 75% in 2025.

### [Fed Holds at 3.5%–3.75%; New Chair Warsh Signals Possible Hike Path](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The Fed held rates steady at its June meeting, but new Chair Kevin Warsh stripped the easing bias from the statement and markets now price a meaningful chance of a hike later in 2026, with inflation still projected above target. That hawkish shift is a notable regime change for a market that spent early 2026 betting on cuts.

## Market Outlook

Equities began the week on the back foot, with the Dow off 0.59% and breadth soft (Russell 2000 -0.67%), as renewed Iran-related airstrikes and a Houthi shipping embargo revived the energy risk premium — though resilient semiconductors kept the Nasdaq roughly flat and the VIX calm below 19. The bigger story is a shifting policy backdrop: with the 10-year yield backing up toward 4.60% and new Fed Chair Warsh dropping the easing bias, the rate-cut tailwind that powered the first half is giving way to genuine hike risk. That matters most into a dense earnings slate, where several watchlist names report within two weeks and ex-dividend dates for COST and PFE land Thursday. The most important near-term catalyst is **Alphabet's report Tuesday**, which opens Big Tech earnings and will test whether AI capex — running into the hundreds of billions across Meta, TSMC, and the hyperscalers — is finally converting to visible returns, a question Chamath and Jamie Dimon are both pressing. With strategists from Tom Lee to Buffett flagging a historically volatile August–October window, the setup warrants attention as the July 28–29 FOMC meeting approaches.
