### [Stocks slide, oil surges as UAE intercepts Iranian missiles](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-may-4-231452685.html) — Yahoo Finance
The Dow shed 557 points after the UAE intercepted Iranian missiles and Israel and Bahrain raised alert levels, reigniting Strait of Hormuz risk premiums. ### [Amazon launches Supply Chain Services, opening logistics network to third parties](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/why-is-united-parcel-service-stock-plunging-today-93CH-4656524) — Investing.com

### [Fed holds at 3.50–3.75% in 8-4 split vote; signals easing bias](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/fed-dissenters-explain-no-votes-saying-they-disagreed-with-hinting-next-move-would-be-a-cut.html) — CNBC
The April 29 FOMC kept funds rates unchanged for a third meeting, but the four-dissent vote — first since October 1992 — exposed deep policy divisions. Governor Miran wanted a 25bp cut; three others objected to language hinting the next move would be lower. Powell separately confirmed he will not seek another Chair term but will remain on the Board.

### [Trump expands Section 232 metals tariffs; Canada unveils C$1.5B relief](https://lethbridgeherald.com/news/national-news/2026/05/04/joly-unveils-1-5-billion-in-tariff-relief-after-trump-ratchets-up-trade-war/) — Lethbridge Herald
The US average effective tariff rate hit 11.8% in April after a surprise expansion of metals tariffs. Trump separately threatened a 25% levy on EU autos, citing non-compliance with the trade deal. Canada's Joly announced C$1.5B in tariff relief targeting Ontario manufacturers and mould-makers.

## Market Outlook

The tape went risk-off but unevenly: Middle East escalation drove a 7.7% VIX pop, oil up 3–5%, and gold surprisingly down 2.2% as the dollar caught a bid alongside crude — a regime that smells more like supply-side panic than classic safe-haven flight. UPS's 10.5% collapse was the day's signature single-stock event, but the bigger story may be Amazon recasting third-party logistics as an AI-adjacent battleground, and the AI infrastructure complex (NVDA flat, TSM +1%, TEM +1.5%) absorbed the macro hit without flinching. Dimon's warning that "the next credit recession would be worse than people think" alongside Dalio's "stagflationary" framing suggests the macro tailwinds for risk assets are getting harder to source. Tuesday's earnings night (PFE, MSTR, SMCI, TEM) will test sector breadth, but Friday's Nonfarm Payrolls — forecast at +73K versus +178K prior — is the week's main event and will dictate whether the Fed's split April vote ratchets toward an actual cut. With four watchlist names near 52-week highs (AAPL, AMZN, GOOG, TSM), the bar for upside surprise is getting higher even as the downside catalysts multiply.
