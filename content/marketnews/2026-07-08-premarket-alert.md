## Overnight Developments

Global markets are in a broad risk-off session. US futures are sharply lower, Asia and Europe sold off overnight, and two distinct catalysts are driving the move: a fresh Middle East military escalation and a semiconductor-led tech pullback out of Asia.

### [Iran ceasefire collapses, US–Iran strikes resume](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-july-8-2026) — TheStreet / CNN
President Trump declared the Iran ceasefire "over" after both sides exchanged strikes overnight. The US reportedly hit 80+ Iranian targets and revoked oil-sale waivers, reimposing sanctions, while Iran's IRGC struck US positions in Bahrain and Kuwait. The escalation drove crude higher and triggered a flight from risk assets.
Affected sectors: Energy, Defense, Financials, Tech.

### [Samsung earnings miss sparks chip selloff](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-sp-500-nasdaq-futures-slip-after-samsung-results-spark-chip-sell-off-223015294.html) — Yahoo Finance
A sharp drop in Samsung shares dragged the semiconductor complex lower across Asia and into US pre-market trading, pressuring the Nasdaq more than the broad market. The weakness explains why tech-heavy futures are leading the decline.
Affected sectors: Technology, Semiconductors.
