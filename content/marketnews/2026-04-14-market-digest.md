## Market Outlook

Markets rallied broadly on Monday as strong bank earnings from JPMorgan and Citigroup combined with cooler-than-expected PPI data — March headline at +0.5% MoM vs 1.1% forecast — to reinforce the disinflation narrative and push the Nasdaq nearly 2% higher. The nuclear and AI infrastructure trade gained renewed momentum, with NNE (+7%), TEM (+6.5%), and OKLO (+6%) surging on UK SMR funding news, while crude oil's sharp 5% decline signals the market is betting on geopolitical de-escalation despite the ongoing US-Iran Strait of Hormuz standoff and fresh 50% tariff threats against China. With TSM reporting Thursday, followed by UNH next week and a wave of mega-cap tech earnings starting April 29 — including GOOG, META, and SOFI — the market's conviction will face its biggest test of the quarter. A sustained move above 7,000 on the S&P 500 warrants attention as a potential breakout signal, but Jamie Dimon's pre-2008 parallels and oil's geopolitical risk premium suggest caution remains warranted.
