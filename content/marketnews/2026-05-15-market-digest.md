## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Trump-Xi Summit Concludes with No Major Trade Deal Breakthroughs](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/u-s-and-china-seek-to-repair-damage-from-tariff-war-that-sent-trade-into-a-freefall) — PBS / CNBC
The Beijing meeting on May 14–15 wrapped with no policy agreements. US tariffs on China remain around 48% after briefly hitting triple digits earlier this cycle, and a federal appeals court paused a lower-court decision that had blocked Trump's 10% global tariff. Markets had priced in a thaw; the absence of one drove today's selling.

### [S&P 500 Falls 1.24% on Yield Spike and Summit Disappointment](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-15-2026-updates) — TheStreet / CNBC
The 10-year Treasury yield jumped roughly 9 bps to 4.55% on renewed inflation jitters, the highest reading in a year. Bright spots were narrow: Cisco surged 13.4% on earnings, and Microsoft rallied after Pershing Square disclosed a position.

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady; Market Pushes Cuts Out to 2027](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve / TheStreet
The April FOMC vote was an unusually contentious 8–4 (first four-dissent meeting since October 1992), with Governor Miran voting for a 25 bp cut. BofA now forecasts no cuts in 2026 and two quarter-point cuts in mid-2027 — a meaningful repricing for duration-sensitive equities.

### [Oil Above $109 on Iran Tensions and Strait of Hormuz Disruptions](https://www.enterprisebank.com/insights/geopolitical-update-may-2026) — Enterprise Bank & Trust
Brent rallied 2.4% to $109.43 as the US-Iran standoff entered its third month and Strait of Hormuz traffic disruptions continued. The geopolitical premium in energy now looks structural rather than cyclical.

## Market Outlook

Today's tape was a clean expression of the new regime: yields up, AI/nuclear/crypto down, defensives mixed, and the dispersion between earnings winners (COST, AAPL, TSM) and the speculative tail (NNE -12%, OKLO -7%, RKLB -6%) widening sharply. The Trump–Xi summit's empty ending plus a 10-year yield breakout to 4.55% reset duration assumptions in real time — and BofA's pivot to "no cuts in 2026" means equity multiples have to do the heavy lifting from here. NVIDIA's earnings Wednesday is now the binary catalyst for the entire AI complex; a beat-and-raise can restart the chase, while any guidance softness collides with Buffett's, Dimon's, and Marks's chorus on stretched valuations and tight spreads. Next week's CPI print is the second tripwire — yields are already moving as if inflation will disappoint. Until those two prints land, momentum favors caution and bias toward quality balance sheets over story stocks.
