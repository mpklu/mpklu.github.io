## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [April CPI Comes In Hot, Nasdaq Sinks](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/12/stock-market-live-may-12-2026-sp-500-spy-sinks-on-higher-inflation/) — 24/7 Wall St.

### [Fed Holds at 3.50%–3.75% on Closest Vote Since 1992](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The April 29 FOMC decision split 8-4. BofA now expects no cuts until late 2027, and the CME FedWatch curve has pushed its next-cut probability into mid-to-late 2027. Kevin Warsh's succession to the Fed chair role is on the calendar for May 15.

### [Court Strikes Down Section 122 Tariffs; Trump Threatens 25% EU Auto Duties](https://www.tradecomplianceresourcehub.com/2026/05/07/trump-2-0-tariff-tracker/) — Trade Compliance Resource Hub
The Court of International Trade invalidated Proclamation 11012 on May 7. The administration responded by threatening to lift EU auto and truck tariffs from 15% to 25%. The average effective U.S. tariff sits at 10.7%, with household impact estimated near $1,500 annually for 2026.

### [Oil Jumps Past $102 on U.S.–Iran Tensions](https://sundayguardianlive.com/business/us-stock-market-today-12-may-2026-dow-dips-nasdaq-sp-500-edge-higher-as-tech-rally-oil-surges-103-on-us-iran-deal-what-investors-should-watch-191547/) — Sunday Guardian
Washington rejected Tehran's counter-offer on sanctions relief and frozen-asset access. WTI jumped 3.3% and Brent 2.6%. Geopolitical strategists are flagging that a sudden resumption of hostilities could trigger a 3–5% S&P 500 drop in a single session.

## Market Outlook

The tape today was bifurcated: defensives held while the most rate-sensitive corners of the market — speculative nuclear small caps (SMR -9.8%, OKLO -5.5%, NNE -5.4%), crypto proxies (MSTR -5.6%, CRCL -5.8%), and post-rally semis (INTC -6.2%) — gave back ground after April CPI printed 3.8%, a three-year high, and WTI jumped past $102 on renewed U.S.-Iran tensions. With the next FOMC still 35 days out and the curve now pricing first cuts into 2027, the path to easing keeps stretching, which warrants caution on duration-sensitive growth names even as megacap earnings continue to validate the AI capex thesis. The most important catalyst from here is NVIDIA's earnings on May 20 — every bullish thesis on the screen, from Ives's Nasdaq 30,000 call to TSMC's raised guide to Druckenmiller's pivot into power infrastructure, ultimately leans on hyperscaler capex holding the line.
