## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Trump announces 50% tariffs on Canadian exports](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/21/trump-trade-tariffs-greer-section-301.html) — CNBC
The administration issued proclamations for 50% duties on a range of Canadian goods (dairy, beer, plywood, hockey equipment), effective in 30 days. Energy, potash, critical minerals, autos, and steel are exempted. Prime Minister Carney is reportedly planning retaliatory measures. The USTR also signaled additional tariff actions against multiple countries "soon," including a proposed 12.5% Section 301 tariff on imports from 60 economies — a meaningful escalation of trade-policy risk.

### [Safe-haven demand grows as US-Iran tensions escalate](https://www.monetamarkets.com/markets/safe-haven-demand-grows-as-geopolitical-risks-shape-global-markets-21st-july-2026/) — Moneta Markets
Reports of a sharp US-Iran military exchange — US strikes and Iranian missiles reportedly hitting bases in the Gulf — pushed crude sharply higher (WTI +2.9%, Brent +3.2%) and lifted gold. Despite the headlines, equities rallied as investors leaned into a strong earnings tape.

### [Fed expected to hold at July 28–29 meeting, but hike risk lingers](https://intellectia.ai/blog/fed-interest-rate-decision-july-2026-analysis) — Intellectia / CME FedWatch
Consensus is for the Fed to hold, but 2026 inflation projections were revised upward (~3.6%) and Governor Waller's recent remarks hint at a pivot toward inflation containment. CME FedWatch shows roughly a 25% probability of a 25bp hike at the upcoming meeting.

## Market Outlook

Equities pushed broadly higher — the S&P 500 +0.89% and NASDAQ +1.29% — with the risk-on tone underscored by the VIX collapsing 8.6% to 17.05, even as WTI (+2.9%) and gold (+0.9%) reflected a live geopolitical premium from US-Iran escalation. The standout was a violent rotation into beaten-down cyclicals and speculative growth: chip and AI-hardware names (INTC +8.1%, TSM +5.3%, SMCI +6.5%) and nuclear/space plays (SMR +8.7%, OKLO +5.5%, RKLB +4.7%) all ripped, suggesting money is chasing laggards ahead of a dense earnings calendar rather than piling further into megacap leaders. This warrants attention because the market is absorbing two competing narratives at once — a strong earnings tape versus a hardening trade-policy stance, with fresh 50% Canadian tariffs and hints of broader Section 301 action. The most important near-term catalyst is the wall of reports now landing: Alphabet tomorrow, Intel Thursday, then a Meta/Amazon/Apple cluster the following week, all feeding into the July 28–29 FOMC decision where a ~25% hike probability keeps the outcome less than fully priced. Momentum favors the bulls into these prints, but the combination of tariff escalation and a Fed that just nudged its inflation outlook higher suggests caution is warranted if earnings fail to justify the rotation.
