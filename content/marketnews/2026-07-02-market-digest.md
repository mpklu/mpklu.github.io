## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [June jobs report misses badly — payrolls +57K vs. +113K expected](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/02/jobs-report-june-2026-.html) — CNBC
The June employment report, released a day early ahead of the holiday, showed nonfarm payrolls rising just **57,000** — roughly half the ~113K consensus and a four-month low. The unemployment rate ticked *down* to **4.2%**, but largely because the labor force shrank (participation fell 0.3pp). Leisure/hospitality shed 61K jobs. A weak-but-not-collapsing print that complicates the Fed's "no cuts" stance without forcing its hand.

### [Fed holds at 3.50%–3.75% and drops easing guidance](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) — Federal Reserve
At its June meeting the Fed left rates unchanged and removed language pointing to cuts, lifting its 2026 PCE inflation projection to 3.6% with several officials now open to a hike. Markets are pricing roughly an **80% probability of zero cuts in 2026** — a hawkish backdrop that keeps pressure on rate-sensitive growth names.

### [USTR advances Section 301 tariffs on 59+ trading partners](https://www.dorsey.com/newsresources/publications/client-alerts/2026/6/new-section-301-tariffs) — Dorsey
A proposed 10%–12.5% tariff structure targeting alleged forced-labor practices across 59 countries and the EU entered its comment period (through July 6, hearing July 7), while a threatened 35% tariff on Canada looms for August 1. Trade policy remains a live tail risk into Q3.

## Market Outlook

The last session before the holiday captured 2026's central tension in miniature: a soft June payrolls print (+57K) coincided with a record Dow close even as chip and mega-cap tech names (INTC −5.4%, META −4.8%, TSM −3.2%) dragged the Nasdaq lower. The rotation into value and defensives, alongside gold's push past $4,180 and a weaker dollar, suggests investors are hedging a stagflationary tilt rather than betting cleanly on rate relief — a reasonable posture with the Fed on hold at 3.50%–3.75% and markets pricing ~80% odds of no 2026 cuts. Idiosyncratic stories dominated the tape (MSTR's buyback-and-Bitcoin pop, SMCI's Taiwan export probe, Circle's stablecoin-competition scare), a hallmark of a stock-picker's market rather than a broad trend. The most important near-term catalysts are the June CPI on July 14 and back-to-back TSM and UnitedHealth earnings on July 16, which will test both the inflation narrative and whether the semiconductor and managed-care leaders can steady sentiment. Momentum favors the defensives until the data forces the Fed's hand either way.
