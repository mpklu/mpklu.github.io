## Market Outlook

Today's tape was a rehearsal for next week's main event: oil's ~2.4% jump on Strait-of-Hormuz anxiety combined with disappointing prints from IBM and ServiceNow was enough to pull the S&P, Nasdaq and Dow lower, even as a handful of watchlist names (COST, UPS, OKLO) bucked the move. Under the surface, the cross-currents are sharpening — AI capex commitments from AMZN/Anthropic and GOOG/Merck continue to expand, while Meta's 10% layoff and SMCI's Oracle-order loss remind us the same spending cycle is forcing brutal rationalization elsewhere. With the FOMC decision (4/28–29), META/GOOG/AMZN/SOFI (4/29), AAPL/PCE (4/30), and UPS (4/28) all landing in a five-trading-day window, dispersion — not direction — is what warrants attention. Positioning into Friday's UMich sentiment print and next week's earnings avalanche suggests caution on crowded mega-cap longs; momentum favors defensives and the nuclear-SMR complex until the macro backdrop clears. Watch Intel's after-close print tonight — a clean beat validates the semis-ex-NVDA rotation already forming.
