## Market Outlook

Markets returned from the long weekend leaning back into risk: the Nasdaq's +1.1% lead and a sub-16 VIX signaled that the AI/semiconductor trade — bruised in late June — is back in favor, helped by a strong Foxconn read and Citi's constructive TSM setup (TSM +4.1%). Beneath the index calm, dispersion stayed wide: Rocket Lab fell 7.3% on Iridium-deal dilution fears, Circle bounced 6.2% as ARK bought its stablecoin-competition dip, and defensives like Pfizer and UnitedHealth lagged the tape. The steady 10-year near 4.49% and gold holding above $4,150 suggest investors are adding exposure without fully unwinding hedges, a reasonable stance with the Fed firmly on hold. The near-term catalysts stack up quickly: June FOMC minutes Wednesday, CPI on July 14, and a pivotal July 16 doubleheader of TSM and UnitedHealth earnings. Momentum favors semis and megacap tech into earnings season, but the inflation and guidance prints will decide whether the rotation has staying power.
