## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Iran Launches Missiles at Kuwait and Bahrain](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/06/03/stock-market-today-june-3-stocks-slip-in-morning-trading-on-fresh-geopolitical-tensions/) — The Motley Fool

This was the primary catalyst behind today's selloff.

### [Trump Administration Proposes Tariffs on 60 Trading Partners](https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2026/06/03/us-tariffs-60-economies-dection-301-forced-labor-trade-practices-.html) — CNBC
The White House proposed Section 301 tariffs of 10% on economies with forced-labor prohibitions and 12.5% on others, targeting China, Brazil, India and more. Select goods (beef, tomatoes, coffee) are exempt, and a comment period precedes implementation — but the proposal layered fresh trade uncertainty onto an already jittery tape.

## Market Outlook

Equities sold off broadly today, with the Dow (-1.21%) and Russell 2000 (-1.31%) leading losses as Iran's missile strikes on Kuwait and Bahrain pushed WTI crude up 1.7% and reignited Strait of Hormuz supply fears. The risk-off tone was amplified by a fresh Trump tariff proposal targeting 60 trading partners, and the damage was concentrated in speculative, cash-burning names — nuclear (NNE -14.9%, SMR -12.0%, OKLO -10.0%) and crypto-adjacent fintech (CRCL -10.4%, MSTR -6.3%, SOFI -5.3%) bore the brunt, while Intel (+6.2% on Computex AI momentum) and Meta (+3.8% on an analyst upgrade) showed that quality AI stories still attract bids. With the VIX still subdued at 16 and the 10-year yield firming to 4.49%, the move reads more as a sentiment-driven derisking than a structural break. Friday's May nonfarm payrolls report (consensus +75,000) now looms as the week's most important catalyst — a soft print would test how much the market is leaning on a still-resilient labor market.
