## Market Outlook

Markets rallied on Iran ceasefire hopes and better-than-expected ISM manufacturing data, but the session told two stories: headline indices moved higher while Nike's 7% plunge and several small-cap names selling off hard suggest the rally remains narrow — a point Jim Cramer and multiple analysts flagged explicitly. The manufacturing prices surge to 78.3 (well above 73 forecast) complicates the Fed's calculus, especially with markets now pricing a 52% probability of a rate hike by year-end rather than a cut. Friday's Non-Farm Payrolls print is the week's key catalyst — consensus expects just 50K jobs (down from 60K prior), and a significant miss could reignite recession fears while a beat would reinforce the stagflation narrative. With earnings season kicking off in earnest mid-April (TSM Apr 16, GE and UNH Apr 21, INTC and GOOG Apr 23), the next three weeks will test whether the AI-led rally can broaden or if Buffett's $382B cash pile proves prescient.
