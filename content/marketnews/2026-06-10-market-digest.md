A broad, high-volume risk-off session. Losses were led by mega-cap tech and AI-infrastructure names, with the VIX jumping nearly 12% to 22.2 — its highest reading in weeks. Notably, gold fell 2.7% alongside equities while Treasury yields and the dollar firmed, a cross-asset signature pointing to a repricing of rate expectations rather than a flight-to-safety event.

## Breaking News & Macro Events

### Hot May CPI revives rate-hike fears ahead of FOMC
May consumer prices came in firmer than expected, with the headline index accelerating month-over-month (CPIAUCSL 333.98 vs. 332.41 prior — roughly +0.47% MoM). With the FOMC meeting just days away (June 16–17), traders repriced the path of policy: the 10-year yield held near 4.54% and the dollar index firmed to ~100, while gold sold off 2.7%. The simultaneous rise in real yields and decline in gold is the tell that this was a rate-driven move, not a fear bid.

### AI-infrastructure capital raises stoke supply-glut worries
A wave of dilutive financings hit AI-adjacent names. **Super Micro (SMCI) cratered ~22%** after announcing a roughly $7B capital plan ($5B underwritten offering plus a $2B ATM program) to fund AI-server backlog. **MicroStrategy (MSTR)** stayed under pressure amid fresh equity and preferred issuance to fund Bitcoin purchases, and nuclear names (NNE, SMR, OKLO) sold off hard. The theme echoes a warning Jim Cramer voiced this week — that bull markets are "most easily killed by excess supply."

## Market Outlook

Equities sold off broadly today — the S&P fell 1.62%, the NASDAQ 1.98%, and the VIX spiked nearly 12% to 22.2 — as a firmer-than-expected May CPI revived rate-hike concerns just ahead of the June 16–17 FOMC meeting. The move bore the hallmarks of a rate repricing rather than a panic: the 10-year held near 4.54% and the dollar firmed while gold fell 2.7%, an unusual pairing that suggests rising real yields, not fear, drove the selling. Compounding the macro pressure, a cluster of dilutive AI-infrastructure financings — Super Micro's ~$7B raise sent the stock down 22%, with MicroStrategy and the nuclear names also hit hard — validated the "excess supply" worry that bulls and bears alike have flagged. With the Fed now in focus, the setup warrants caution: markets are leaning toward a hawkish hold, and any signal of an actual hike could extend the pressure. Watch this week's PPI and jobless-claims prints, then Powell's tone next Wednesday, for the next directional cue.
