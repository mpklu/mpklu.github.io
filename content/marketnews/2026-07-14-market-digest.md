## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Inflation Cools, Trimming Fed Rate-Hike Odds](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/treasuries-rally-as-cool-cpi-data-cuts-july-fed-hike-bets-to-20) — Bloomberg
A softer-than-expected June CPI print (index down ~0.4% month-over-month, per FRED data through June) eased fears of a near-term Fed rate hike and sent Treasuries higher. Market-implied odds of a July move fell sharply, reinforcing expectations the Fed holds steady at its July 28–29 meeting. The relief drove the risk-on tone, with the VIX sliding nearly 4% to 16.5.

### [Trump Drops Strait of Hormuz Transit Fee, Pivots to Trade Deals](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-07-14/trump-and-antidumping-tariffs) — Bloomberg
The administration abandoned a proposed 20% protection fee on Strait of Hormuz transit, replacing it with trade and investment agreements with Gulf states. The resolution of an acute supply-chain uncertainty capped what had been a building geopolitical risk premium, though crude finished only modestly higher (WTI +0.28%).

## Market Outlook

Stocks drifted higher as a cooler-than-expected June CPI print eased near-term rate-hike fears, with the Nasdaq (+0.90%) leading on semiconductor strength — NVIDIA (+4.2%) and Intel (+5.2%) both rallied — while the VIX slid nearly 4% to 16.5. The softer inflation reading gives the Fed room to hold steady into its July 28–29 meeting, and the risk-on tone favored high-beta AI and nuclear names; still, a flat Dow (+0.02%) and weakness in defensives like UnitedHealth and Pfizer show the advance was narrow rather than broad-based. The next two weeks are unusually catalyst-dense: TSMC and UnitedHealth report July 16, Alphabet follows July 22, and the FOMC decision lands July 28–29. With valuations rich and figures from Jamie Dimon to Tom Lee cautioning on froth, the setup warrants attention — momentum favors tech, but the earnings-and-Fed gauntlet ahead leaves little room for disappointment.
