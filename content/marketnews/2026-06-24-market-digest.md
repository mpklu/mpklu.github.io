## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed holds rates, signals a possible hike — not a cut](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html) — CNBC
The FOMC held the funds rate at 3.50–3.75% on June 17, but updated projections show most policymakers now expect a rate *hike* later in 2026 as inflation runs above target. That hawkish pivot reframes every upcoming inflation print as a potential catalyst rather than a formality.

### [Trump administration relaunches tariff agenda via forced-labor rules](https://www.aljazeera.com/amp/news/2026/6/15/how-trump-is-relaunching-a-tariff-war-citing-forced-labour-concerns) — Al Jazeera
USTR announced Section 301 tariffs of up to 12.5% on 60 economies — including developed allies — citing forced-labor concerns. The current universal 10% tariff expires July 24 and remains under legal challenge, with hearings scheduled for July 7.

### [Middle East tensions reshape energy markets](https://www.gurufocus.com/news/8895580/middle-east-oil-supply-faces-major-disruptions-amid-geopolitical-tensions) — GuruFocus
Iran–Israel friction and Houthi Red Sea disruptions are pressuring supply forecasts, yet crude *fell* hard today (WTI -3.6%, Brent -4.2%) as markets price a benign, short-lived scenario. The gap between the risk narrative and the price action is itself worth watching.

## Market Outlook

Beneath a quiet headline tape — the S&P off a tenth of a percent, the Dow modestly green — the day was anything but calm under the surface: Strategy (MSTR) cratered 10% on a Bitcoin slide, Rocket Lab fell nearly 10% as the SpaceX IPO pressured the whole space complex, and nuclear names (OKLO, SMR, NNE) sold off together, while Tempus AI bucked the trend with a 6% gain on FDA validation. The split tells a familiar story: capital is punishing leverage, narrative, and dilution (SMCI's $7B raise) while rewarding tangible catalysts, and the VIX's 4% drop says this was rotation, not panic. With the Fed having signaled a possible *hike* rather than a cut, the burden of proof now sits squarely on the inflation data. Thursday's Core PCE print warrants close attention — a hot reading would harden the hawkish tilt and keep the pressure on rate-sensitive, long-duration growth. Watch whether the speculative unwind stays contained to crypto, space, and nuclear, or begins to bleed into the broader AI trade.
