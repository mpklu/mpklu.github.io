## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Intel double-upgraded, chip complex rips higher](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/INTC) — CNBC
Bank of America issued a double upgrade to Buy on Intel, citing improved foundry visibility, and reports of an Alphabet AI-chip manufacturing deal sent INTC up over 11%. The move rippled across the semiconductor complex (TSM +3.4%, NVDA +2.9%, SMCI +11.3%), reinforcing AI-infrastructure as the market's leadership group.

### [Oil tumbles ~6% as supply fears ease](https://www.businessupturn.com/sectors/commodities/mcx-commodities-on-june-11-crude-rises-0-50-on-iran-escalation-as-gold-silver-slip-on-hot-us-inflation-data) — Business Upturn
WTI fell 6.1% and Brent 6.1% as Middle East de-escalation hopes pulled risk premium out of crude. Falling energy prices acted as a tailwind for equities and helped compress the VIX, even as gold rose 3.4% — a sign some investors kept a hedge on despite the rally.

### [US–China set 60-day tariff pause](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/09/trump-tariffs-trade-china-forced-labor.html) — CNBC
A reported 60-day pause leaves existing tariffs (roughly 30% combined) in place but halts further escalation, removing a near-term overhang. A separate Section 301 forced-labor proposal targeting 59 countries remains a longer-dated risk worth monitoring.

## Market Outlook

Today was a clean risk-on rally with strong internals: the Russell 2000 (+3.0%) led, the VIX fell 12.5% below 20, and a ~6% drop in crude eased the inflation/supply overhang that has dogged markets — with semiconductors, led by Intel's 11% surge on a BofA double-upgrade and a reported Google foundry deal, doing the heavy lifting. The combination of falling oil, a softer dollar, and a 60-day US–China tariff pause points to genuine risk appetite rather than a narrow melt-up, though gold's 3.4% gain and still-firm CPI (333.98 vs 332.41) suggest investors aren't fully letting their guard down. That tension is echoed in the commentary spread — Ives and Lee see more upside while Cramer, Dalio, and Buffett warn on supply of stock and stretched valuations. Tomorrow's May PPI print is the next near-term catalyst: a hot reading would test today's conviction that disinflation and Fed patience can coexist with the AI-capex boom. With the next FOMC still 47 days out, price action — not policy — will set the tone into mid-month, and breadth this strong warrants attention as a signal worth watching.
