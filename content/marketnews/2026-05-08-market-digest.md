## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Strong April Jobs Report Lifts Markets](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-8-2026-updates) — TheStreet
The U.S. economy added **115,000 jobs in April** versus a 65,000 consensus, with unemployment steady at 4.3%. The upside surprise reinforced a soft-landing narrative without forcing the Fed's hand on the rate path, and equities pushed both the S&P 500 and Nasdaq to record closes.

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady; Powell Era Ends May 15](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/fed-interest-rate-decision-april-2026.html) — CNBC
The April FOMC kept the target range at **3.50–3.75%** in an 8–4 split vote. Chair Jerome Powell's term ends May 15; Trump nominee **Kevin Warsh** is set to take over and is widely expected to favor cuts, a regime shift markets are starting to price into the front end of the curve.

### [Tech Sector Drives 6-Week Win Streak](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/05/08/stock-market-today-live-coverage/) — The Motley Fool
The Nasdaq has tacked on **+4.5% over the streak**, with AMD crossing $700B in market cap (up 90% in a month) on AI compute demand. An AWS thermal incident briefly disrupted Coinbase and FanDuel — a reminder of cloud concentration risk under heavy AI workloads.

## Market Outlook

A clean-print April jobs report (115K vs 65K consensus) gave the bulls cover to push both the S&P 500 and Nasdaq to record closes, with a sixth-straight weekly gain riding on AI infrastructure spend (NVDA $5.17T, AMD $700B, Intel breaking out on Apple manufacturing talks) and a 28% blowout from RKLB. The dispersion is loud — UNH and Apple at the highs, Pfizer and Nike near the lows, MSTR rallying on Bitcoin while Dimon and Dalio openly warn on valuations and credit. Next week is dense: CRCL, HIMS, OKLO and NNE all print earnings within four trading days, **CPI lands Tuesday and PPI Wednesday**, and Kevin Warsh formally takes the Fed chair on May 15 with markets already discounting a more dovish bias. The single biggest catalyst on the horizon remains **NVDA earnings on May 20**, which will set the tone for the AI-cap-spend trade that has carried this entire tape. Momentum favors the bulls into the inflation prints, but with CPI breadth narrowing and named voices flagging concentration risk, position sizing — not direction — is what warrants the most attention here.
