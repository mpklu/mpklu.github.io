## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed Holds at 3.5%–3.75%, Removes 2026 Cut Guidance](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The FOMC left the funds rate unchanged at its June meeting and stripped out earlier guidance for a 2026 cut, pushing reductions into 2027–2028 as Iran-conflict-driven inflation lingers. Traders are now pricing in a possible hike as early as October — a meaningful hawkish repricing that keeps the front end of the curve elevated.

### [Micron Crushes Earnings, Qualcomm Lifts Long-Term Outlook](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) — CNBC
Micron jumped ~17% on a blowout quarter ($25.11 adjusted EPS vs. $20.78 expected; revenue quadrupled YoY to $41.46B), and Qualcomm rose ~9% after doubling its 2029 non-handset revenue target to $40B. The memory and AI-silicon complex remains the market's strongest fundamental story even as valuations wobble.

### [Tariff Talks Intensify; Canada Sets July 21 Counter-Tariff Deadline](https://ustr.gov/trade-topics/presidential-tariff-actions) — USTR
US and Canada pledged at the G7 to reach a deal within 30 days, but Ottawa warned it will raise counter-tariffs on steel and aluminum on July 21 if talks stall. The US trade chief is in India working to close a "very close" pact. Tariffs now represent the largest US tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993 — roughly $1,500 per household in 2026.

## Market Outlook

Today was a rotation, not a retreat: the Dow and Russell 2000 closed green while mega-cap tech sagged, led by Apple's 5.4% drop on China weakness and device price hikes, and a brutal session for speculative nuclear and crypto-proxy names (NNE -13%, MSTR -12%, OKLO -7.6%, RKLB -7%). The split tape — paired with falling NVDA GPU lease rates and a softer Nasdaq now down four straight — suggests investors are trimming the most stretched, narrative-driven positions while rotating toward cyclicals and defensives like UPS and UNH. With the Fed having stripped its 2026 cut guidance and traders flirting with an October hike, the macro backdrop warrants caution on rate-sensitive, long-duration speculation. The most important near-term catalysts are Nike's earnings on June 30 and Alphabet's June 29 Dow entry, with the July 28–29 FOMC meeting looming as the next true inflection point.
