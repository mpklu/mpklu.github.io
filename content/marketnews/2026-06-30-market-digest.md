## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [US-Iran Hostilities Cease, Strait of Hormuz Reopened](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-30-2026) — TheStreet
The US and Iran agreed to halt hostilities and restore safe passage for commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz, removing a major overhang. The relief sparked a tech and semiconductor rally and helped push the VIX down nearly 7% to 16.45.

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady, Removes Forward Guidance](https://www.usbank.com/investing/financial-perspectives/market-news/federal-reserve-interest-rate.html) — U.S. Bank
At its June meeting under new Chair Kevin Warsh, the Fed held the funds rate for a fourth straight time and dropped forward guidance. Notably, FOMC members now forecast inflation rising toward 3.6% annualized by year-end (up from 2.7% in March), and markets are increasingly pricing a possible hike later in 2026.

### [Trump Tariffs on Forced-Labor Practices Expand to 60 Economies](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/06/03/trump-administration-announces-new-tariffs-over-use-forced-labor/) — Washington Post
The USTR proposed additional 10%–12.5% tariffs on 60 economies tied to forced-labor practices, affecting China, the EU, and Japan. The 2026 tariff slate is shaping up as the largest US tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993.

## Market Outlook

Markets closed a strong quarter on a decisively risk-on note: the S&P 500 rose 0.79%, the Nasdaq jumped 1.52%, and the VIX fell nearly 7% to 16.45 as the US-Iran ceasefire and reopening of the Strait of Hormuz removed a key overhang. The leadership was unmistakably AI-and-semiconductor driven — TSM surged 5.1% to a fresh 52-week high on a Barclays target hike to $625, Intel rose 6.0% toward its own 52-week high, and Apple and NVIDIA added 2.9% and 2.6% — even as crypto-linked names diverged sharply, with Circle down 17.6% on the Visa/Stripe "Open Standard" stablecoin threat and Strategy off 6.9%. The split warrants attention: relief buying and AI-capex momentum are powering index gains, but the Fed's upward revision to its year-end inflation path (toward 3.6%) and growing odds of a 2026 hike suggest the macro backdrop is less benign than the tape implies. The most important catalyst ahead is the July 28–29 FOMC meeting, with June PPI on July 15 serving as the next read on whether that inflation worry is justified.
