## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [S&P 500 posts third straight loss amid rising Treasury yields](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-18-2026-updates) — TheStreet
Third consecutive down session as the 30-year Treasury yield touched 5.198%, its highest mark in nearly two decades. The bond market is doing the tightening the Fed is no longer doing — equity multiples are taking the hit.

### [Fed holds rates steady at 3.5%–3.75% amid inflation concerns](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/) — Federal Reserve
Newly confirmed Fed Chair Kevin Warsh continues to hold the line. BofA now pushes first rate cuts into July–September 2027, citing energy-driven inflation tied to Iran tensions. The "higher for longer" thesis is hardening into "higher for much longer."

### [U.S.–China trade tensions remain elevated; negotiations show temporary stabilization](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/u-s-and-china-seek-to-repair-damage-from-tariff-war-that-sent-trade-into-a-freefall) — PBS News

### [Semiconductor sector under pressure ahead of Nvidia earnings](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) — CNBC
Large speculators in NDX futures are now sitting on the biggest net short position since 2023. Whatever Nvidia prints tomorrow afternoon, the positioning is asymmetric.

## Market Outlook

A third straight down day in equities is now coinciding with a 30-year Treasury yield at its highest in nearly two decades and a positioning that's net short Nasdaq futures at 2023 extremes — the bond market is doing the policy tightening the Fed has stepped away from, and equity multiples are absorbing the bill. The gold-down/equities-down/dollar-up signature points to deleveraging rather than a clean macro rotation, while elevated oil reflects Iran-driven supply tail risk. Notable voices are bifurcating sharply: Ives, Lee, and Wood see another leg up driven by AI capex, while Dimon, Dalio, and Buffett are explicitly flagging excess and waiting for a bigger drawdown. **Tomorrow's Nvidia print is the singular catalyst** — with speculators heavily short and analyst expectations of $1.77 EPS and $79.1B in revenue, the asymmetric setup favors a sharp move in either direction. Watch the 10-year yield through 4.70% and Friday's jobs report for confirmation of whether this pullback finds a floor or extends.
