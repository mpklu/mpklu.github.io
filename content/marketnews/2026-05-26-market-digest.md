## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Brent Crude Plunges 6%+ on US–Iran Ceasefire Optimism](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-oil-05-20-2026/) — Fortune
Brent crashed to $96.47/bbl, the lowest since April, as the US and Iran discussed extending a two-month ceasefire that would lift the US blockade and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. WTI followed lower (-3.07%). Energy weakness was the day's most consequential macro signal — softening inflation expectations while removing a key geopolitical tail risk.

### [Stocks Extend Streak to 8 Weeks on Iran Peace Progress](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/05/26/stock-market-today-may-26-micron-surges-after-ubs-lifts-price-target-on-ai-optimism/) — Motley Fool / CNBC
Risk-on rally continued: S&P 500 closed at a record after eight consecutive weekly gains, NASDAQ added 1.19%, and the 10-year yield slipped to 4.49%. Micron surged 16% on a UBS upgrade, reinforcing the AI memory super-cycle narrative. Small caps (Russell 2000 +1.79%) and nuclear/space names led the breadth expansion.

### [Trump Sets July 4 Deadline for EU Trade Deal, Threatens 25% Auto Tariffs](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
Administration gave the EU until July 4 to ratify the framework deal or face 25% tariffs on autos and trucks. Tariffs collectively now represent the largest US tax-as-share-of-GDP increase since 1993 (~$1,500/household). Auto names and exporters remain in the line of fire heading into summer.

### [New Fed Chair Warsh Begins Tenure; Markets Price In Hike Risk](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
CME FedWatch shows ~70% odds of at least one 0.25% hike by year-end under Chair Kevin Warsh, who replaced Powell on May 23. The hawkish framing collides with Trump's rate-cut campaign — setting up a high-stakes June FOMC.

## Market Outlook

A textbook risk-on day: Iran ceasefire progress crushed Brent (-6.83%) and dragged the 10-year yield back below 4.5%, fueling an eighth straight weekly S&P 500 gain and a broad NASDAQ-led rally with small caps (+1.79%) and nuclear/space leaders (RKLB to fresh highs, NNE/SMR/OKLO all up 5–10%) leading the breadth. The setup matters because cheaper energy plus tame yields is the cleanest possible runway into Friday's Core PCE — the print that effectively sets the tone for the June 16 FOMC under new Chair Warsh, whose hawkish reputation has rates markets pricing in hike risk despite political pressure to cut. Costco's Thursday earnings is the most actionable single-name catalyst this week, with options implying a ~3.7% move and the stock already down 2.4% on de-risking. The bullish tape now leans hard on two assumptions — that Iran de-escalation holds and PCE confirms disinflation — and both warrant attention given the tariff and geopolitical wildcards still in play heading into June.
