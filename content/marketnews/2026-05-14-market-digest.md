## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Senate Confirms Kevin Warsh as New Fed Chair](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/05/14/warsh-be-confirmed-fed-chair-trump-allies-warn-rate-cuts/) — Washington Post
Kevin Warsh was confirmed in a 54-45 Senate vote, taking the helm of the Federal Reserve amid expectations for policy shifts. Trump allies are already warning that rate cuts may have to wait given today's PPI surprise and lingering inflation concerns — markets had been pricing in a faster easing path.

### [Trump-Xi Summit Begins: Tariff Truce in Focus](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/14/trump-xi-summit-us-china-trade-taiwan-iran-nvidia.html) — CNBC
Presidents Trump and Xi met in Beijing for bilateral talks, with analysts watching for a tariff-truce extension and Boeing aircraft purchases. Mentions of NVIDIA and Taiwan put semiconductors squarely in the negotiating frame — TSM (+4.0%) and NVDA (+3.5%) responded accordingly.

### [Federal Court Strikes Down Trump's 10% Global Tariffs](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
The U.S. Court of International Trade ruled Trump's 10% global tariffs under Section 122 of the Trade Act unlawful. Trump has responded by threatening higher tariffs on the EU if a trade deal isn't ratified by July 4, keeping trade-policy uncertainty front and center.

### [Iran War Supply Chain Disruptions Exceed Tariff Impacts](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/trump-xi-summit-china-iran-war-tariffs.html) — CNBC
Supply-chain disruption from the 11-week Iran war is now inflicting more damage on the global economy than tariffs, choking shipping lanes and keeping crude prices elevated (WTI back above $101). The longer the conflict runs, the harder it becomes to dismiss the energy-shock channel.

### [S&P 500 Surpasses 7,500 on Tech, Cerebras Debut Pops 68%](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-may-14-2026-sp-futures-rise-after-closing-at-record-high) — TheStreet
S&P 500 and NASDAQ printed record closes as AI-chip newcomer Cerebras debuted at a $95B market cap (+68% on day one). Cisco and other tech earnings kept the AI infrastructure trade firing.

## Market Outlook

Today's tape was a study in cognitive dissonance: a +1.4% PPI surprise that should have rattled bonds and a confirmed new Fed chair whose mandate is unclear barely dented an S&P 500 closing at a fresh 7,500 record, while NVDA pushed past $5.5T and TSM tagged an all-time high on TSMC's upgraded $1.5T-by-2030 chip-market call. What it means: AI infrastructure leadership is being treated as immune to rate trajectory, and the gap between the bull voices (Ives, Lee, Wood) and the warning voices (Buffett, Dalio, Dimon) is now as wide as it has been all cycle — Buffett's record $397B cash pile is the most concrete expression of that gap. Crude above $100 from the Iran war and a tariff regime that just had its global 10% pillar struck down by a federal court keep the macro tape genuinely two-sided beneath the surface. The most important upcoming catalyst is NVIDIA's earnings on May 20 — at a fresh ATH with the Trump-Xi summit explicitly touching on NVIDIA and Taiwan, that print will warrant attention as the next stress test of the AI-capex thesis.
