### [Hawkish Fed Repricing Triggers Risk-Off Selloff](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-23-2026) — TheStreet / BofA

## Market Outlook

Today was a clean risk-off session: the NASDAQ fell 2.2% and the VIX jumped nearly 13% as a hawkish Fed repricing collided with a sharp semiconductor unwind, while the defensive-tilted Dow held nearly flat. The trigger was less about any single data point than a shift in narrative — a BofA note raising the specter of 2026 rate hikes landed on a chip complex already nervous about debt-financed AI capex, dragging TSMC (-6.7%), Super Micro (-8.3%), and NVIDIA (-3.8%) lower and spreading globally as Korea's KOSPI dropped almost 10%. The bifurcation within our watchlist is telling: defensives and idiosyncratic stories (UNH at its 52-week high, SOFI, TEM) closed green while anything levered to AI, crypto, or nuclear growth sold off hard, with MSTR notching a fresh 52-week low. The 10-year held steady near 4.49% and gold eased, suggesting this was a growth-and-positioning scare rather than an inflation shock. Thursday's PCE/CPI inflation print now warrants close attention — a hot number would validate the hawkish repricing and pressure rate-sensitive growth further, while a soft one could quickly stabilize sentiment.
