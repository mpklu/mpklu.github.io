## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed holds rates steady in historic 8-4 vote with four dissenters](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/fed-interest-rate-decision-april-2026.html) — CNBC
The FOMC kept the target range at **3.50–3.75%** in Powell's final meeting as chair, but the vote split 8-4 — the most dissent since October 1992. Governor Miran wanted a 25bp cut; three others objected to language hinting at future easing. The statement explicitly cited inflation pressures from rising global energy prices tied to the Iran conflict, signaling the Fed sees a stagflationary mix it can't dovishly resolve.

### [Powell to remain on Board of Governors after chair term ends May 15](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/fed-meeting-today-live-updates-warsh-powell.html) — CNBC
Jay Powell announced he will stay on the Federal Reserve Board indefinitely after his chair term expires May 15, citing the ongoing investigation into the central bank's headquarters renovation and what he described as Trump's legal pressure leaving him "no choice." The decision keeps a hawkish institutional voice on the FOMC even as Kevin Warsh prepares to take the chair.

### [Trump prepares extended blockade of Iran; oil prices surge](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-apr-29-2026-update) — The Street / WSJ
WSJ reported the administration has told aides to prepare for an extended naval blockade of Iran, sending crude sharply higher for a second day. **WTI jumped +8.82%** to $108.10 and Brent +7.17% to $111.53. Energy is now up roughly 40% YTD and is increasingly the dominant driver of headline inflation.

### [Section 122 global 10% tariff in effect through July 24, 2026](https://www.tradecomplianceresourcehub.com/2026/04/19/trump-2-0-tariff-tracker/) — Trade Compliance Resource Hub
After the Supreme Court struck down the IEEPA-based tariffs, the administration invoked Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 to apply a 150-day global 10% tariff (expiring July 24), with stated intent to step up to 15%. Steel, aluminum, and copper articles remain at 50%, derivatives at 25%. The administration also threatened additional tariffs on UK imports unless London drops its 2% digital services tax.

### [Mega-cap tech earnings: Alphabet, Amazon, Meta after the bell](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/28/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) — CNBC
Three of the Magnificent Seven report Q1 results after Wednesday's close, with SoFi already out and tech-heavy tape sitting on tight ranges into the print. The reaction sets the tone for May and will arbitrate whether the AI capex narrative still clears the higher-yield, higher-oil bar.

### [Pershing Square IPO prices at low end, raises $5B](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/04/29/stock-market-today-live-coverage/) — Motley Fool
Bill Ackman's Pershing Square Capital Management debuted Wednesday raising $5B in its combined IPO, pricing at the **low end** of expectations — a soft tape signal as a wave of mega-IPOs (OpenAI, SpaceX, Anthropic) lines up to drain liquidity from the secondary market.

## Market Outlook

The tape today is best described as **stalled at a hinge point**: equities barely budged at the index level, but the internals tell a stagflationary story — VIX up 5.5%, oil up nearly 9%, the dollar firming, and a historic 8-4 FOMC dissent making it harder for either bulls or bears to hold conviction. Powell's exit from the chair (while staying on the Board) and the Section 122 tariff regime running through July layer geopolitical and policy risk on top of a market already paying full multiples for a soft-landing outcome that the Fed itself just signaled is now contested.

The dispersion across the watchlist warrants attention: **INTC's +10.2% blowout** validates the AI infrastructure thesis at the underdog end of the chip stack, while **SOFI's -15.9% reset** and the broad nuclear-basket selloff (NNE, OKLO, SMR each down 4-7%) suggest momentum favors quality over story stocks heading into the heart of earnings. With AMZN, GOOG, META, AAPL all reporting in the next 24 hours and PCE landing tomorrow, the next two sessions are likely to set the tone for May — pay closer attention to capex and margin commentary than to top-line beats.
