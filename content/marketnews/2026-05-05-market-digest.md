## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed Signals Easing Bias; Four Dissents Over Rate Cut Language](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The FOMC held rates at 3.50–3.75% but inserted dovish easing-bias language that drew an unusually wide four-dissent split from the Dallas, Cleveland, and Minneapolis presidents. Kevin Warsh is set to take the chair by mid-June, layering personnel risk on top of a divided committee.

### [Canada Unveils $1.5B Tariff Relief After US Metal Tariff Expansion](https://www.nationalobserver.com/2026/05/04/news/joly-unveils-15-billion-tariff-relief-after-trump-ratchets-trade-war) — National Observer
Ottawa is deploying $1.5B in relief as expanded US steel, aluminum, and copper-derivative tariffs land on Canadian manufacturers — some facing tariff bills as high as $600K per shipment. The 10% universal tariff regime continues to ripple through North American supply chains.

### [Apple Eyes Intel as Part of Multi-Vendor Chip Strategy](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/intel-intc-stock-apple-talks-report.html) — CNBC
Reports that Apple is in exploratory talks with Intel and Samsung for US-based processor production sent INTC up 13% to a new all-time high and pressured TSM (-1.4%). The story dovetails with Apple's stated push to diversify away from Taiwan-concentrated supply.

### [Amazon Opens Logistics Network to Third Parties](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/05/05/massive-news-for-amazon-stock-investors/) — The Motley Fool
Amazon Supply Chain Services launched May 4 with P&G, 3M, and American Eagle as early customers, putting Amazon's 6.7B-package domestic network in direct competition with UPS (4.4B) and FedEx. UPS held up better than feared today (+2.0%) on what may already be priced-in fears.

## Market Outlook

Equities pushed to fresh records on a tape that combined a softer crude print, a relenting bid for safety, and a melt-up in semis catalyzed by the Apple–Intel rumor — a single headline that simultaneously reset INTC's narrative and trimmed TSM. Beneath the rally, the Fed's four-dissent statement and Buffett's $397B cash pile are reminders that the easing-bias trade and the valuation-risk trade are happening in the same market, not different ones. The week's pivot point is Friday's NFP into Michigan sentiment: a soft payroll print would harden cuts-by-summer pricing, but a hot wages number would test how much of today's small-cap bid is real conviction versus short-cover. Watchlist-specific risk is unusually concentrated tonight — PFE, MSTR, SMCI, and TEM all report on a single tape — and Thursday brings RKLB and SMR. The next 72 hours will show whether the rally has earnings to back it up, or whether it was a one-day rotation in search of a thesis.
