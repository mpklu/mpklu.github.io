## Market Outlook

Today was a textbook risk-off cascade: a semiconductor-led slide dragged the NASDAQ down **4.18%**, the VIX spiked nearly **40%**, and even traditional havens like gold (-2.9%) sold off as investors raised cash across the board. The proximate trigger was a one-two punch — soft chip-sector sentiment colliding with a **hot May payrolls print (172K vs. 85K expected)** that pushes the Fed's easing timeline further out and pressures the most rate-sensitive, highest-multiple corners of the market. The breadth of the damage — AI infrastructure, nuclear, crypto-adjacent, and fintech names all down high single to double digits, while defensive UnitedHealth and Pfizer closed green — suggests a positioning unwind in crowded growth trades rather than a fundamental break. With **CPI on June 10, PPI on June 11, and an FOMC meeting now just 11 days out (June 16–17)**, the path forward hinges on whether next week's inflation data validates or eases the "higher-for-longer" fear that drove today's repricing. The setup warrants caution into the data, but the relative resilience of mega-cap quality names is worth watching as a tell on whether this is a flush or the start of something deeper.
