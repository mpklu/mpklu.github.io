## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady, Removes Easing Bias](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The Fed left its benchmark unchanged (Fed funds effective rate near 3.63%) but stripped its prior easing-bias language, and updated projections show a sizable bloc of officials now penciling in a potential hike later in 2026 rather than cuts. The pivot toward "higher-for-longer" reframes the bullish tape: equities rallied today, but rate-sensitive assets repriced, with gold off 2.4% and the dollar firmer.

### [Stocks Surge on Fed Hold and Easing US-Iran Tensions](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-18-2026) — TheStreet
A broad risk-on session sent the S&P 500 and Nasdaq sharply higher on the Fed decision and reports of a U.S.-Iran de-escalation framework. Semiconductors led the advance while defense names and oil/gas lagged. Today was also the last session before the Juneteenth holiday, coinciding with a triple-witching expiration and the S&P quarterly rebalance.

### [Trump Administration Launches New Tariff Push](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/15/how-trump-is-relaunching-a-tariff-war-citing-forced-labour-concerns) — Al Jazeera / USTR
The USTR is advancing tariffs on 60+ economies citing forced-labor concerns, with 16 economies — including Canada, Mexico, the EU, Taiwan, and the UK — facing proposed 10% levies. A public comment period runs through July 6 with hearings July 7, keeping trade policy a live overhang for import-exposed sectors.

### [Geopolitical Risk Elevated as Strait of Hormuz Remains in Focus](https://www.blackrock.com/corporate/insights/blackrock-investment-institute/interactive-charts/geopolitical-risk-dashboard) — BlackRock
Iran-related tensions and Strait of Hormuz dependency keep energy and defense allocations in flux even as today's headlines leaned constructive. BlackRock flags geoeconomic confrontation as 2026's top global risk, a reminder that the de-escalation narrative remains fragile.

## Market Outlook

A textbook risk-on session: the S&P rose 1.08%, the Nasdaq 1.91%, and the Russell 2000 2.12% as the VIX collapsed 11% on the Fed's hold and an apparent easing of U.S.-Iran tensions — with semiconductors carrying the tape (TSM +5.7%, Intel +7.8% on a reported Apple foundry partnership). But the rally deserves an asterisk: the Fed scrapped its easing bias and a growing bloc of officials now sees a possible 2026 hike, and gold's 2.4% drop alongside a firmer dollar shows the market quietly repricing toward higher-for-longer. With U.S. markets closed Friday for Juneteenth and today's triple-witching adding noise, conviction behind the move warrants some caution. The next real catalysts are Nike's June 30 earnings, the July 6 close of the tariff comment period, and the July 28 FOMC — any of which could test whether today's optimism has legs.
