## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Dell surges ~33% on earnings beat as the AI trade rolls on](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/stock-market-news-may-29-132100817.html) — Yahoo Finance
Dell's blowout Q1 guidance ($43.8B revenue vs. ~$34.8B consensus) lit a fire under the AI infrastructure complex, dragging Super Micro and server names higher. The result reinforces that the data-center capex cycle remains intact, and it was the day's dominant driver of risk appetite.

### [Appeals court stays ruling against Trump's 10% global tariff](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/5/7/us-trade-court-rules-against-trumps-10-percent-global-tariffs) — Al Jazeera
A U.S. trade court rejected the across-the-board 10% tariff under Section 122, but a federal appeals court paused that decision pending review. The Section 122 authority is capped at 150 days and expires in July unless Congress acts, keeping trade policy a live overhang for import-exposed names like Nike.

### [Fed stays on hold; "higher for longer" hardens](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The FOMC held rates at its April meeting (third straight hold) on an 8-4 vote — the most dissents since 1992 — with inflation still running near 3.3%. Several desks now see no cuts until well into 2027, a backdrop that keeps the long end firm and pressures rate-sensitive corners of the market.

### [Iran de-escalation hopes ease the energy risk premium](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-28/asian-stocks-to-rise-on-firming-us-iran-sentiment-markets-wrap) — Bloomberg
Progress toward an Iran ceasefire pulled crude off recent highs, with WTI and Brent both easing on profit-taking after a sharp run-up tied to Strait of Hormuz disruption fears. Lower geopolitical premium is a tailwind for equities and a headwind for the energy sector that led the year.

## Market Outlook

U.S. equities ground to fresh records as Dell's blowout AI guidance reignited the data-center trade, lifting the Dow 0.72% and dragging Super Micro and the semiconductor complex higher — but the soft Russell 2000 and a sub-16 VIX underscore how narrow and complacent this leadership has become. Costco's near-4% drop on an earnings *beat* is the tell: with megacap valuations stretched and the Fed signaling no cuts until well into 2027, the bar for these leaders is unforgiving, and even strong results can disappoint. That tension is echoed by the unusually cautious chorus of voices this week — Dimon, Buffett, and Dalio all flagging exuberant valuations while Druckenmiller quietly rotates within the AI trade rather than out of it. The setup warrants attention more than alarm: momentum favors the AI-infrastructure names, but the cushion is thin. The most important near-term catalysts are the June 16-17 FOMC meeting with fresh projections and the July expiry of the Section 122 tariff authority — either could reset a market that is currently priced for everything to go right.
