## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [FOMC Decision and May CPI Both Land Tomorrow](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm) — Federal Reserve
The Fed's June meeting (June 16–17) concludes tomorrow with an updated Summary of Economic Projections, and May CPI prints the same day. With the funds rate parked near 3.63% and core inflation still sticky, the dot plot and Powell's tone are the market's primary near-term catalyst. Today's rally suggests positioning for a dovish-or-steady read.

### [US Plans New Tariffs on 60+ Economies Citing Forced-Labor Concerns](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/u-s-says-it-plans-extra-tariffs-of-10-or-more-for-most-trading-partners-after-forced-labor-probe) — PBS News
The administration outlined proposed levies of 10% on 16 economies (Canada, Mexico, EU, Taiwan, UK) and 12.5% on 44 others (China, Japan, India, South Korea, Switzerland), pending public hearings beginning July 7. A live tail risk for import-heavy and semiconductor supply chains, though implementation is not yet final.

### Reported US–Iran De-escalation Adds to Risk Appetite
Multiple market reports pointed to a reported US–Iran interim agreement and a potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, cited as a tailwind for risk assets and AI-infrastructure names. Worth flagging the caveat: crude rose modestly today (WTI +0.94%) rather than falling, so treat the de-escalation narrative as unconfirmed and watch oil for confirmation.

## Market Outlook

A broad risk-on rally swept the tape, with the NASDAQ surging 3.07% and the VIX collapsing 8.4% to 16.20 as AI infrastructure (NVDA +3.4%, TSM +3.7%, META +4.3%) and speculative growth (HIMS +12%, nuclear names +5–7%) led. The move reflects a reassertion of the AI-capex narrative — TSMC raising 3nm pricing, NVIDIA's $20B inaugural bond at an upgraded AA rating, and Super Micro closing $7B in financing all point to durable demand — layered on top of reported geopolitical de-escalation, though the modest rise in crude warrants caution on how firm that thaw really is. The setup suggests momentum favors growth into the close of the week, but the conviction is borrowed against tomorrow. Both the FOMC decision (with fresh projections) and May CPI land June 16–17, and a hot inflation print or hawkish dot plot could unwind today's enthusiasm quickly. Watch tomorrow's data as the single most important catalyst — with the proposed tariffs on 60+ economies sitting as an unresolved tail risk beneath the rally.
