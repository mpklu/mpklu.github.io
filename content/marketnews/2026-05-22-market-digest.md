## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Trade Court Rejects Trump's Section 122 Global Tariffs](https://ourtake.bakerbotts.com/post/102ms7t/trump-tariff-tracker-may-8-2026) — Baker Botts
A divided U.S. Court of International Trade panel ruled on May 7 that President Trump's 10% global tariffs imposed under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 were unlawful, rejecting an expansive reading that would have allowed Section 122 to function as a catch-all tariff power. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent has indicated tariffs could return to prior levels by early July through other authorities, keeping trade policy on investors' watch list.

### [EU Auto Tariffs Could Spike to 25% in July](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
The administration is threatening to raise tariffs on European autos and trucks to 25% (or "much higher") in July if the EU fails to ratify the trade deal negotiated last year. The escalation risk is a key overhang for European exporters and auto suppliers.

### [PPI Surges to 6% YoY in April, Trimming Rate-Cut Hopes](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/13/economy/us-ppi-wholesale-inflation-april) — CNN Business
Wholesale prices accelerated to 6% on an annual basis in April, well above expectations and up from 4% in March. Markets have scaled back rate-cut expectations and now expect no additional easing in 2026 following three Fed cuts in late 2025. Tariff pass-through is being flagged as a key driver.

## Market Outlook

Markets closed the pre-Memorial Day session with the **Dow at a record high** and broad participation, but the leadership mix told a more nuanced story: nuclear power, AI-infrastructure beneficiaries (RKLB, SMCI, NNE) and defensive healthcare (UNH) outpaced mega-cap tech, while NVDA, MSTR and AMZN gave back ground. The combination of a record Dow and the highest-profile bears — Dimon, Buffett, Druckenmiller — warning about "exuberance," casino-like behavior and bubble narratives **warrants attention** as the rally widens past the 2025 cut cycle. Next Thursday's GDP-second-read and PCE deflator print, landing on a holiday-shortened week and into the June 16–17 FOMC, is the most important catalyst on the calendar; with markets now pricing zero further easing in 2026, an upside inflation surprise is the asymmetric risk.
