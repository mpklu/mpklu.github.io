## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed holds rates, drops easing bias under new chair Warsh](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html) — CNBC
Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting as chair ended with rates held at 3.50%–3.75%, but the statement scrapped its bias toward cuts and the updated dot plot erased a 2026 cut, pushing easing out to 2027–2028. Nine officials now pencil in at least one hike, with markets pricing potential tightening as early as October. A hawkish Fed reset is the single biggest weight on the AI-momentum trade right now.

### [US proposes Section 301 tariffs on 60 economies over forced-labor practices](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/us-tariffs-60-economies-dection-301-forced-labor-trade-practices-.html) — CNBC
The USTR found 60 countries failed to adequately restrict forced-labor goods and proposed new tariffs, layered on top of a fresh 25% levy on certain Brazilian goods and a proposed suspension of the de minimis duty exemption. The escalation keeps trade-policy risk front and center for import-heavy sectors.

### [Tech volatility deepens as OpenAI reportedly delays IPO to 2027](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-26-2026) — TheStreet
A global tech sell-off pressured megacap AI names on reports OpenAI is pushing its IPO to 2027, reviving valuation and data-center-cost concerns. Capital rotated into defensives — healthcare led, with UnitedHealth at a fresh 52-week high. Separately, Alphabet joins the Dow Jones Industrial Average before the open on June 29, replacing Verizon.

## Market Outlook

Equities finished little changed and mixed, with the S&P 500 (-0.05%) and NASDAQ (-0.24%) pinned by a global tech sell-off — sparked by reports of an OpenAI IPO delay — while a fading VIX (18.41) and a rotation into healthcare (UnitedHealth to a fresh 52-week high) cushioned the tape. The bigger story is regime change: a more hawkish Fed under new chair Kevin Warsh that has erased 2026 cut expectations, layered onto escalating tariff actions and a lingering Iran energy premium that pushed gold up 1.58% to $4,082. That mix warrants caution toward the most stretched AI-momentum names — evident in Strategy's slide to a fresh low and Nano Nuclear's slump — even as semiconductor bulls like Tom Lee and Dan Ives read the pullback as a buyable reset. Today's PCE inflation print and Nike's June 30 earnings are the near-term catalysts, but the dominant question heading into the July 28–29 FOMC is whether the Fed's hike risk hardens or fades. Momentum favors defensives and quality until the inflation and rate picture clarifies.
