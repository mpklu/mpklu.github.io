## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Semiconductor sell-off deepens as AI trade wobbles](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/07/17/stock-market-midday-july-17-netflix-plunges-and-stocks-slide-as-semiconductor-sell-off-deepens/) — The Motley Fool

### [US-Iran tensions push oil above $80](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-july-17-2026) — TheStreet
Escalating Middle East conflict lifted WTI crude ~3% past $80/barrel, with markets watching Strait of Hormuz closure risk that could disrupt roughly 20% of global oil flows. Firmer energy prices complicate the inflation picture just ahead of the July 28–29 FOMC.

### [Netflix falls 10% on revenue miss](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/stock-market-today-july-17-165830273.html) — Yahoo Finance
Netflix beat on EPS ($0.80) but missed revenue expectations ($12.56B vs. ~$12.6B consensus), sending shares down 10% and adding to tech-sector weakness. The reaction underscored how unforgiving the tape has become for high-multiple names.

### [Tariff escalation: Brazil 25%, Canada 35%](https://www.riotimesonline.com/usa-canada-intelligence-brief-friday-july-17-2026/) — Rio Times
Washington's 25% tariffs on Brazilian goods take effect July 22, while a fresh executive order lifts tariffs on Canadian lumber and auto parts to 35%. The Canadian dollar slid to a two-year low near 0.72, keeping trade policy a live risk-off catalyst.

## Market Outlook

Equities extended their chip-driven decline into a broad risk-off Friday, with the S&P 500 down 1.0%, the Nasdaq off 1.4%, and the VIX spiking above 18 as the semiconductor gauge logged its worst week since April 2025 — even TSMC's blowout quarter and raised >40% growth guide couldn't stem the AI-fatigue selling. The weakness broadened beyond chips into mega-cap tech (GOOG -3.0%, META -3.1%) and was compounded by Netflix's 10% revenue-miss drop, fresh Brazil/Canada tariff escalation, and oil jumping past $80 on Middle East tensions — a mix that keeps the inflation-and-policy backdrop uncomfortable. With several watchlist names sitting near 52-week lows (NNE, OKLO, SMR) and heavyweight earnings from Alphabet (July 22) and Intel (July 23) landing before the July 28–29 FOMC, momentum favors quality and cash flow over speculative story stocks. The confluence of a live rate-hike debate and the start of Big Tech earnings warrants caution and close attention in the days ahead. Watch whether the chip complex stabilizes or drags the tape lower as the AI-monetization question comes to a head.
