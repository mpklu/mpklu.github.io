## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [S&P 500 breaks 7,600 as the AI trade splits in two](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-02-2026) — TheStreet

But the rally is narrowing: software names (Atlassian, ServiceNow, Salesforce) tumbled on AI-replacement fears, and Alphabet's record $80B equity offering weighed on sentiment over dilution and the sheer scale of AI capex.

### [US imposes 25% tariff on Brazilian goods](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/6/2/us-targets-brazil-with-new-tariffs-over-trade-practices) — Al Jazeera
The administration set a new 25% tariff on Brazilian imports (replacing last year's 50%), citing digital-trade and deforestation disputes. The effective US tariff rate now averages ~11.8%, the largest tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993 — keeping trade friction firmly on the radar.

### [Fed holds; markets price no cuts until 2027](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressrelease/monetary20260429a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The Fed funds rate stays at 3.5–3.75% (FRED: 3.63%). With inflation still above target and energy prices elevated, Bank of America now sees the Fed on hold through 2026, pushing the first cut to mid-2027. Rate-path clarity remains the scarce commodity.

## Market Outlook

Markets ground out modest gains as the S&P 500 closed above 7,600 for the first time, but the headline number masks a market splitting cleanly into haves and have-nots. The AI trade is fracturing in real time: TSMC and Apple pressed against 52-week highs on NVIDIA's Computex momentum, while Alphabet fell 3.1% on its record $80B equity raise and MicroStrategy cratered 8.8% after its first Bitcoin sale since 2022 — a dispersion that suggests the market will no longer reward anything short of perfection. The split shows up in the commentary too, with a cautious Buffett (sitting on $397B cash) and a wary Cramer on one side, and an emphatically bullish Tom Lee on the other. With the Fed firmly on hold, fresh 25% tariffs on Brazil, and crude grinding higher on Hormuz disruption, sticky inflation remains the binding constraint on any dovish pivot. Watch May PPI on June 11 and the June 16–17 FOMC meeting — now 14 days out — as the next catalysts for whether rate-cut hopes survive into the second half.
