## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [S&P 500 Notches Fresh All-Time High Despite Hot PPI Print](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-13-2026-updates) — TheStreet
April PPI came in at **6.0% YoY** — well above the **~3.0% consensus** and a stark divergence from the 3.8% CPI print. Megacap tech absorbed the inflation surprise: Apple touched **$300/share**, the S&P 500 set a new record at 7,444, and the Nasdaq tacked on **1.2%**. The 10-year Treasury rose to **4.48%**, its highest since mid-2025, signaling bond markets are taking the producer-price spike seriously even as equities shrug.

### [US Court Pauses Decision Blocking Trump's 10% Global Tariff](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/5/12/us-court-pauses-decision-blocking-trumps-10-percent-global-tariff) — Al Jazeera
An appeals court issued an administrative stay of the Court of International Trade's 2-1 ruling against the administration's Section 122 tariffs. The stay gives the White House time to respond; **the ~48% effective rate on China imports remains in place** for now. The legal whipsaw injects fresh uncertainty into supply-chain planning.

### [Trump-Xi Summit Underway in Beijing](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/trump-xi-summit-china-iran-war-tariffs.html) — CNBC
Trump and Xi are meeting in Beijing this week. Notably, Chinese exporters report that **supply-chain disruption from the Iran war is now a bigger concern than tariffs**. Markets are watching for any extension of the trade truce beyond November.

### [Fed Holds at 3.5%–3.75%; Warsh Confirmation Expected May 15](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/05/11/warsh-fed-pressure-inflation/) — Washington Post
Bank of America now sees **no rate cuts in 2026**, with the next easing pushed to H2 2027. With Kevin Warsh's confirmation as Fed Chair imminent and inflation re-accelerating, today's PPI print materially complicates the path forward.

## Market Outlook

Equities punched to fresh highs even as April PPI came in at **6.0% YoY** — double the consensus and a clear divergence from the 3.8% CPI print — with the rally narrowly led by AI megacaps (Apple touched $300, Alphabet +3.4%, NVIDIA +2.9% above its prior 52-week high) while the Dow drifted red. The PPI shock and the **10-year yield at 4.48%** suggest the bond market is taking inflation re-acceleration seriously even as equity buyers focus on AI capex flows; the gap between concentrated-mega-cap strength and broad-market hesitation warrants attention. Today's tape also showed clear stress underneath — SOFI tumbling on accounting concerns, MSTR off 3% with crypto, and Nike pinned near 52-week lows on a Wells Fargo downgrade — reminders that dispersion is widening. **NVIDIA earnings on May 20** are now the single biggest near-term catalyst, with the appeals-court tariff stay and the Trump-Xi summit in Beijing as parallel macro variables. Momentum favors the AI complex into earnings, but the inflation backdrop and Fed re-pricing leave little margin for disappointment.
