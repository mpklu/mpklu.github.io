## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [US–Iran Clashes Pause; Strait of Hormuz Shipping Resumes](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-june-29-224230573.html) — Yahoo Finance
Renewed US–Iran military strikes over the weekend rattled energy markets before both sides agreed to pause hostilities and allow commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz. WTI crude firmed about 0.7% to $70.52. The truce capped what could have been a sharper oil spike, but the episode keeps a geopolitical risk premium in the energy complex.

### [Canada Cancels Digital Services Tax to Restart US Trade Talks](https://www.tradecomplianceresourcehub.com/2026/06/03/trump-2-0-tariff-tracker/) — Trade Compliance Resource Hub
Ottawa scrapped its Digital Services Tax to resume stalled negotiations with Washington, defusing a tariff threat that had been set to take effect June 30. The de-escalation removes one near-term overhang for US tech multinationals.

### [Fed Has Dropped Rate-Cut Guidance, Tilts Hawkish](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html) — CNBC
At its June 17 meeting the FOMC held the funds rate at 3.50%–3.75% but removed prior guidance for a cut this year and nudged its year-end median projection to 3.8%. With sticky inflation, markets are now weighing the possibility of hikes rather than cuts into year-end — a backdrop that makes Thursday's jobs report unusually consequential.

## Market Outlook

Equities staged a clean risk-on rally to start the week, with the NASDAQ up 2.07% and the Dow crossing 52,000 for the first time as Alphabet joined the index and TSMC's 5–10% price hikes reaffirmed semiconductor pricing power. The strength was narrow, however — the Russell 2000 finished flat — and it comes against a Fed that has quietly dropped its rate-cut guidance while a paused-but-unresolved US–Iran conflict keeps a premium under oil. That combination of stretched mega-cap leadership and a hawkish policy tilt warrants attention rather than chase. The most important near-term catalyst is the June jobs report, pulled forward to Thursday ahead of the July 4 holiday: a hot print would harden the case for higher-for-longer and test today's optimism, while Nike's earnings tomorrow offer an early read on the consumer.
