## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Court Strikes Down Second Round of Trump Tariffs](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/07/nx-s1-5815343/trade-court-strikes-down-10-percent-tariffs) — NPR
The U.S. Court of International Trade ruled the administration's 10% global tariffs under Section 122 unlawful, following February's Supreme Court decision that struck down the broader double-digit tariff regime. The court found the balance-of-payments deficit condition required for Section 122 invocation does not currently exist. Margin tailwind for tariff-exposed importers (NKE, AAPL); legal uncertainty continues to fog supply-chain planning.

### [EU Auto Tariff Threat Returns](https://www.bakerbotts.com/thought-leadership/publications/2026/may/trump-tariff-tracker--may-8-2026) — Baker Botts
Trump threatened to raise auto tariffs to 25% in July if the EU does not ratify last year's trade deal by July 4, with potential to escalate further. Auto and luxury supply chains watching carefully.

### [Markets Raise Odds of a Fed Rate Hike](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/markets-raise-chances-for-a-fed-rate-hike-following-hot-inflation-report) — CNBC
Market-implied probability of a 2026 rate hike climbed to ~37% after April CPI. The April FOMC vote was not unanimous: Governor Miran dissented for a 25bp cut while three others objected to the "hold" language — the first 8-4 dissent split since October 1992. Energy-driven inflation from the Iran conflict is keeping core readings above target.

### [BofA Pushes Rate Cuts Out to 2027](https://www.thestreet.com/fed/bofa-drops-blunt-warning-about-fed-rate-cuts-for-remaining-of-2026) — TheStreet
BofA Global Research now expects the Fed on hold for the rest of 2026, shifting two quarter-point cuts to July and September 2027. Persistent inflation and energy costs cited.

### [Iran Tensions Keep Oil Elevated](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-18-2026-updates) — TheStreet
WTI hovering around $101, Brent around $108, with Middle East conflict reducing global LNG supply by ~20% and constraining Strait of Hormuz traffic. The energy backdrop is the proximate reason the Fed's cutting path keeps slipping.

## Market Outlook

A quiet headline tape masked sharp dispersion underneath: HIMS down 10%, MSTR/SMR/OKLO each off more than 5%, while RKLB ripped +6.89% on its blowout Q1 and UNH added 4% on the continued earnings recovery — exactly the "dispersion is the story" market Howard Marks described. The macro backdrop is hardening rather than easing: April's hot CPI pushed Fed-hike odds to 37%, BofA has officially punted rate cuts to 2027, and oil holding the low $100s on Iran tensions is the channel keeping the Fed defensive. That sets up Wednesday's NVDA print as the single biggest near-term catalyst, with the bar elevated by hyperscaler capex commitments approaching $725B but sentiment fragile after three of the last four post-earnings sell-offs. Costco's earnings next week and Thursday's FOMC minutes round out the immediate calendar, but until NVDA reports, this tape rewards selectivity over thematic broad strokes.
