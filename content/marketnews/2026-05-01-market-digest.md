## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Fed Holds Rates Steady, Signals Potential Cuts Ahead](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/fed-dissenters-explain-no-votes-saying-they-disagreed-with-hinting-next-move-would-be-a-cut.html) — CNBC
The Federal Reserve held the federal funds rate at 3.50%–3.75% for a third consecutive meeting, with forward guidance hinting at potential cuts. Four dissents — the highest count since 1992 — pushed back against the easing bias, citing core inflation back at 3.2% (highest since November 2023). This is Powell's final meeting as Chair before Kevin Warsh takes over in June.

### [Trump's Section 301 Tariff Plan Faces Congressional Pushback](https://foreignpolicy.com/2026/05/01/trump-tariffs-ustr-iran-plan-b/) — Foreign Policy
After the Supreme Court struck down the administration's initial global tariffs, the White House is rolling out a Section 301 framework: a 10% global baseline (effective until July 24), 50% on steel/aluminum/copper articles, and 25% on derivatives. Framework agreements with the EU, Japan, South Korea, and UK have been negotiated to soften the blow.

### [Iran Peace Proposal Eases Oil Risk Premium](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-friday-may-1-records-apple-iran-231056146.html) — Yahoo Finance
Iran shared a new proposal to end the conflict via Pakistani mediators. WTI crude eased toward $103/bbl on the news, though Morgan Stanley notes the supply-disruption tail risk could still spike prices to $150+ if talks collapse.

### [Core Inflation Climbs to Highest Level Since November 2023](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/01/business/fed-reserve-rate-hikes) — CNN Business
March data put core inflation at 3.2%, driven by global energy prices. The Fed cited this re-acceleration alongside soft job gains as justification for staying on hold — and dissenters used it to argue against signaling cuts.

## Market Outlook

Friday's tape was a study in dispersion: Apple's record quarter and easing Iran tensions powered the S&P 500 and NASDAQ to fresh highs, while the DOW slipped on industrial weakness and core inflation back at 3.2% kept four FOMC dissenters loud. The setup heading into next week is binary — a Monday/Tuesday earnings cluster (PFE, MSTR, SMCI, TEM all on May 5) lands directly into Friday's nonfarm payrolls, with a Pfizer ex-dividend (May 7) and Meta-style AI-capex risk overhanging every print. With Buffett, Marks, and Dimon all flagging valuation and bond-market fragility while Ives, Wood, and Lee stay constructive, the dispersion warrants attention rather than conviction in either direction. Watch the jobs report and core inflation reaction in two-year yields — those will dictate whether the Fed's "potential cuts ahead" guidance survives the next two weeks.
