## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Trump Administration Revamps Tariff Strategy After Court Setback](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/15/how-trump-is-relaunching-a-tariff-war-citing-forced-labour-concerns) — Al Jazeera / Tax Foundation

### [Fed Rate-Path Debate Intensifies Ahead of September Meeting](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/-a-july-rate-hike-from-the-fed-the-odds-are-rising.html) — CNBC
With the next FOMC meeting not until September 15–16, officials remain divided on the path forward. June CPI cooled to 332.568 from 333.979 — the first monthly decline this cycle, helped by falling gasoline — but some policymakers still flag upside inflation risk. The 10-year yield eased to 4.55%, and the VIX's slide to 15.67 signals markets are comfortable with a Fed on hold for now.

### [ASML Raises 2026 Guidance on "Extremely Strong" AI Chip Demand](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/15/asml-2q-earnings-ai-chips-orders.html) — CNBC
ASML lifted its full-year outlook for the second time this year on booming order intake, planning a 30% capacity expansion in both EUV and DUV immersion lithography. The read-through is bullish for the semiconductor supply chain — a tailwind for TSMC and NVIDIA heading into a dense earnings stretch.

## Market Outlook

Megacap technology carried the tape higher, with Apple punching to a fresh record and the Nasdaq's +0.62% leading the majors as the VIX slid roughly 5% to 15.67. The advance stayed narrow and AI-centric — Apple, Alphabet, Meta, and Amazon all gained around 3% — even as Intel dropped 5% and rate-sensitive nuclear and space names (SMR, OKLO, NNE, RKLB) sank, a bifurcation that mirrors the froth-versus-value debate splitting investor commentary between cautious voices (Buffett, Dimon) and constructive ones (Lee, Cramer). Cooler June CPI and an easing 10-year yield give the bulls cover, but escalating tariff policy and a still-divided Fed warrant attention beneath the calm surface. The most important near-term catalyst arrives tomorrow, when TSMC and UnitedHealth report — followed by Alphabet next week — a stretch that will test whether AI-capex enthusiasm is matched by fundamentals.
