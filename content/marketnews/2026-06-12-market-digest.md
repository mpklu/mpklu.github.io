## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [SpaceX IPO soars ~19% in market debut](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/12/spacex-ipo-spcx-live-updates.html) — CNBC
SpaceX debuted on the Nasdaq under ticker SPCX, jumping roughly 19% from its IPO price. The blockbuster listing reshuffled the entire space-launch trade and hammered incumbents — Rocket Lab (RKLB) fell nearly 16% on the day as capital rotated toward the new entrant.

### [US–Iran peace signals push oil lower, lift equities](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-11/asian-stocks-to-gain-as-trump-signals-us-iran-deal-markets-wrap) — Bloomberg
Diplomatic progress between the US and Iran drove crude below $85 (WTI -2.4%), easing inflation concerns and supporting equities. Lower energy costs were a key tailwind for the broad-market gains and the sharp VIX decline.

### [Fed expected to hold at June 16–17 meeting](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm) — Federal Reserve
With the next FOMC decision just days away, markets are pricing in no change to the policy rate amid a steady 4.3% unemployment rate and contained inflation. The 10-year Treasury yield eased to 4.45%, reflecting a data-dependent, wait-and-see posture.

### [USTR opens Section 301 investigations on forced-labor imports](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/) — Tax Foundation
The US Trade Representative initiated new Section 301 investigations into import violations, layering onto ongoing tariff adjustments across steel, aluminum, and copper. A medium-impact development worth monitoring for supply-chain-sensitive names.

## Market Outlook

Stocks closed broadly higher Friday — the S&P 500 added 0.50% and the Dow 0.70% — as a sharp drop in oil on US–Iran de-escalation sent the VIX tumbling more than 9% to 17.68. Beneath the calm, leadership was narrow and rotational: Rocket Lab cratered nearly 16% as SpaceX's debut reshaped the space trade, crypto-linked names like Circle and MicroStrategy came under pressure, and momentum favorites such as Hims & Hers sold off, even as Intel surged 5% on a Bank of America double-upgrade. The tug-of-war between bulls citing AI monetization (Ives, Lee) and skeptics warning of froth (Cramer, Dalio, Buffett) warrants attention as valuations stay rich. The most important near-term catalyst is next week's June 16–17 FOMC meeting, where a hold is widely expected — leaving the Fed's tone, not the rate itself, as the signal to watch.
