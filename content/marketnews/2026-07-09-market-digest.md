## Breaking News & Macro Events

### [Trump Announces 50% Tariffs on Brazil](https://ustr.gov/trade-topics/presidential-tariff-actions) — USTR / Bloomberg
The administration announced a 50% tariff on Brazilian exports effective August 1, alongside new tariff rates on 14 countries — including Japan and South Korea — that take effect the same day following the July 9 deadline reset. Trade policy is back at the top of the risk stack heading into August, with the potential to reignite input-cost and supply-chain pressure across industrials and consumer names.

### [Escalating US–Iran Military Conflict](https://www.cnbc.com/video/2026/07/09/key-geopolitical-risk-to-the-markets-isnt-iran-its-russia-analyst.html) — CNBC
US strikes on Iranian targets drew retaliatory attacks on US-allied Middle East countries, keeping a geopolitical risk premium in crude. Yet oil actually fell hard today — WTI −3.4%, Brent −3.6% — suggesting the market is pricing ample supply and discounting a Strait of Hormuz disruption, at least until the situation escalates further.

### [Fed Maintains Higher-for-Longer Stance](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) — Federal Reserve
The Fed is holding at 3.50–3.75% with a hawkish tone, and market expectations have solidified around no change at the July 28–29 meeting. With inflation still elevated, policymakers continue to signal patience rather than cuts.

## Market Outlook

Equities climbed broadly today — the NASDAQ up 1.3% and the VIX back under 16 — as semiconductor strength and a resilient risk appetite overpowered fresh tariff headlines and Middle East escalation. The more telling signal was beneath the surface: gold rallied while crude tumbled more than 3%, a divergence that suggests investors are pricing trade-policy and growth risk over an oil-supply shock, even with US–Iran tensions live. With the Fed firmly in higher-for-longer mode ahead of its July 28 meeting, the near-term tape hinges on hard data and earnings rather than policy. The most important catalyst arrives fast: next week brings June CPI and PPI on July 15, followed by TSMC and UnitedHealth earnings on July 16 — the first real read on whether the AI-capex and healthcare narratives can carry the second half. Momentum favors the bulls for now, but the August 1 tariff deadline warrants attention.
